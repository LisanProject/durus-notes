Chapter 2: Aqsam al-Kalam

Having defined its own basic terms in Chapter 1, the text turns briefly to the structure of Arabic speech itself, since every later chapter, command, prohibition, general and specific wording, and so on, depends on being able to correctly classify a piece of speech first. This chapter covers the minimum building blocks of a sentence, the major categories speech falls into, and the distinction between literal and figurative usage.

Here is a list of the sections covered in this chapter.

The Structure of Speech: The minimum combinations of words that can form complete speech, and the seven categories speech divides into.

Haqiqa and Majaz: Literal versus figurative usage, and the four types of figurative usage, each illustrated with a real Quranic example.
