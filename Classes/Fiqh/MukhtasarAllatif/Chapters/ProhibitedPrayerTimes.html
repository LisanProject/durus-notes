Prohibited Times for Prayer أوقات النهي

Text
وَتَحْرُمُ الصَّلَاةُ فِي خَمْسَةِ أَوْقَاتٍ: عِنْدَ طُلُوعِ الشَّمْسِ حَتَّى تَرْتَفِعَ قَدْرَ رُمْحٍ، وَعِنْدَ الِاسْتِوَاءِ فِي غَيْرِ يَوْمِ الْجُمُعَةِ حَتَّى تَزُولَ، وَعِنْدَ الِاصْفِرَارِ حَتَّى تَغْرُبَ، وَبَعْدَ صَلَاةِ الصُّبْحِ حَتَّى تَطْلُعَ، وَبَعْدَ صَلَاةِ الْعَصْرِ حَتَّى تَغْرُبَ، وَلَا يَحْرُمُ فِيهَا مَا لَهُ سَبَبٌ فِي الْحَالِ، كَتَحِيَّةِ الْمَسْجِدِ وَالْكُسُوفِ دُونَ رَكْعَتَيِ الِاسْتِخَارَةِ، وَيَحْرُمُ ابْتِدَاءُ الصَّلَاةِ بَعْدَ صُعُودِ الْخَطِيبِ فِي يَوْمِ الْجُمُعَةِ عَلَى الْمِنْبَرِ غَيْرَ التَّحِيَّةِ.
Prayer is forbidden at five times: at sunrise until it rises the height of a spear, at the meridian on a day other than Friday until it declines, when it turns yellow until it sets, after the dawn prayer until sunrise, and after the 'Asr prayer until sunset. Nothing with a present cause is forbidden during these times, such as the greeting of the mosque and the eclipse prayer, but this does not extend to the two rak'ahs of Istikhara. And it is forbidden to begin any prayer, other than the greeting, after the preacher has ascended the pulpit on Friday.

Mukhtasar al-Latif, on the five prohibited times.

The Five Prohibited Times

Prayer is forbidden at five specific times.

1. When the sun is rising, until it ascends a spear's length above the horizon.

(1)

2. When the sun is at its zenith (highest point), except on Fridays.

(2)

3. When the sun turns yellow, until it sets.

(3)

4. After the 'Asr prayer, until sunset.

(4)

5. After the Fajr prayer, until sunrise.

(5)

Exception: Prayers with an Immediate Cause (Sabab)

A prayer whose cause is present at that exact moment is permitted even during a prohibited time. Tahiyyat al-Masjid, the prayer greeting the mosque upon entering, and Salat al-Kusuf, the eclipse prayer, are the standard examples, since in both cases the reason for the prayer exists right then and there.

This does not extend to Istikhara, the prayer of consultation, even though it is also a prayer tied to a specific decision. Istikhara's cause, the decision being contemplated, does not require praying at that exact moment, so it is not permitted during one of the five prohibited times the way Tahiyyat al-Masjid or Salat al-Kusuf would be.

The Friday Khutbah Restriction

Once the khatib ascends the minbar for the Friday sermon, it becomes forbidden to begin any prayer other than the greeting of the mosque. A prayer begun after this point, aside from that one exception, is not permitted.

Exception: Masjid al-Haram

The general prohibition on supererogatory prayer during the five forbidden times does not apply within the Sacred Mosque in Makkah. Voluntary prayer remains permitted there even at times it would otherwise be disallowed elsewhere.
