Hadith 4: The Stages of Creation

Text
عَنْ أَبِي عَبْدِ الرَّحْمَنِ عَبْدِ اللَّهِ بْنِ مَسْعُودٍ رَضِيَ اللَّهُ عَنْهُ قَالَ: حَدَّثَنَا رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ وَهُوَ الصَّادِقُ الْمَصْدُوقُ: إِنَّ أَحَدَكُمْ يُجْمَعُ خَلْقُهُ فِي بَطْنِ أُمِّهِ أَرْبَعِينَ يَوْمًا نُطْفَةً، ثُمَّ يَكُونُ عَلَقَةً مِثْلَ ذَلِكَ، ثُمَّ يَكُونُ مُضْغَةً مِثْلَ ذَلِكَ، ثُمَّ يُرْسَلُ إِلَيْهِ الْمَلَكُ فَيَنْفُخُ فِيهِ الرُّوحَ، وَيُؤْمَرُ بِأَرْبَعِ كَلِمَاتٍ: بِكَتْبِ رِزْقِهِ، وَأَجَلِهِ، وَعَمَلِهِ، وَشَقِيٌّ أَوْ سَعِيدٌ. فَوَاللَّهِ الَّذِي لَا إِلَهَ غَيْرُهُ، إِنَّ أَحَدَكُمْ لَيَعْمَلُ بِعَمَلِ أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ حَتَّى مَا يَكُونُ بَيْنَهُ وَبَيْنَهَا إِلَّا ذِرَاعٌ، فَيَسْبِقُ عَلَيْهِ الْكِتَابُ فَيَعْمَلُ بِعَمَلِ أَهْلِ النَّارِ فَيَدْخُلُهَا، وَإِنَّ أَحَدَكُمْ لَيَعْمَلُ بِعَمَلِ أَهْلِ النَّارِ حَتَّى مَا يَكُونُ بَيْنَهُ وَبَيْنَهَا إِلَّا ذِرَاعٌ، فَيَسْبِقُ عَلَيْهِ الْكِتَابُ فَيَعْمَلُ بِعَمَلِ أَهْلِ الْجَنَّةِ فَيَدْخُلُهَا. رَوَاهُ الْبُخَارِيُّ وَمُسْلِمٌ.
'Abdullah ibn Mas'ud said: The Messenger of Allah ﷺ, the truthful, the trusted, related to us: "Each of you is gathered in the womb of his mother for forty days as a drop, then he is a clot of blood for a similar period, then a lump of flesh for a similar period, then the angel is sent to him and breathes the soul into him, and is commanded with four matters: to write his provision, his lifespan, his deeds, and whether he will be wretched or blessed. By Allah, other than whom there is no god, one of you may perform the deeds of the people of Paradise until there is only an arm's length between him and it, and then what has been written overtakes him, and he performs the deeds of the people of the Fire and enters it. And one of you may perform the deeds of the people of the Fire until there is only an arm's length between him and it, and then what has been written overtakes him, and he performs the deeds of the people of Paradise and enters it." Narrated by al-Bukhari and Muslim.

Narrated by 'Abdullah ibn Mas'ud; recorded in Sahih al-Bukhari and Sahih Muslim.

The Three Forty-Day Stages

The hadith describes creation unfolding across three sequential periods of forty days each, one hundred twenty days in total, before the ensoulment described in the next stage occurs.

1. Nutfah (Drop): the first forty-day period.

(1)

2. 'Alaqah (Clot): the second forty-day period.

(2)

3. Mudghah (Lump of Flesh): the third forty-day period, after which the angel is sent.

(3)

The Angel and the Four Decrees

After the three stages, an angel is sent to breathe the soul (ruh) into the body, and is commanded to record four specific matters at that moment: the person's provision (rizq), lifespan (ajal), deeds ('amal), and whether they will ultimately be wretched or blessed (shaqi aw sa'id).

The blowing of the soul is treated as a divine secret (sirr ilahi). Human knowledge of the biological and embryological stages leading up to that moment can be studied and described in real detail, but the nature of the soul itself, and the fixing of a person's ultimate destiny at that same moment, remain fundamentally beyond human access, however much is eventually understood about the physical stages surrounding it.

Debate: When Does the Soul Enter?

Scholars have debated exactly when ensoulment occurs within this general hundred-twenty-day framework, with some placing it as early as ninety days and others holding to the full one hundred twenty. The point of broadest practical agreement is the one hundred twenty day mark: past this point, there is consensus that abortion is no longer permissible, since the fetus is considered ensouled by then regardless of which side of the earlier debate one takes.

Divine Decree and Moral Responsibility

The hadith's closing image, someone an arm's length from Paradise who is then overtaken by what was written and enters the Fire instead, and the reverse case, is a direct statement about qadar (divine decree): a person's final state was already recorded before their deeds unfolded, in a way no human being can access in advance.

This is used to affirm that all good ultimately comes from Allah, and a person's final destiny rests on Allah's prior knowledge rather than a mechanical tally of a life's final visible moments. At the same time, this does not erase moral responsibility: a person remains accountable through their intention and through what is called kasb (acquisition) of their acts, meaning they genuinely will and perform their actions, even though those actions were already known to Allah beforehand. The tension between decree and responsibility is held, not resolved away in either direction: a person is neither excused by qadar nor operating outside of it.

Question 1A fetus is at day 100 of development. According to the hadith's three-stage structure, which stage is it in, and has ensoulment necessarily occurred yet on the majority understanding?

Day 100 falls within the third stage, mudghah (lump of flesh), which spans days 81 to 120. Whether ensoulment has occurred yet is exactly the point of scholarly debate: some place it as early as day 90, which would mean it has already occurred by day 100, while others hold to the full 120 days, which would mean it has not yet occurred. The point of firm consensus is only that by day 120, ensoulment has certainly occurred.

Question 2A person hears this hadith and concludes: "If my final state is already written, then my choices don't actually matter." Using the concept of kasb, explain what is wrong with this conclusion.

This conclusion collapses the distinction the hadith is holding in tension. Allah's prior knowledge of a person's final state does not erase the reality that the person genuinely wills and performs their own actions, which is what kasb (acquisition) refers to. A person remains morally accountable through their intention and their actual acquisition of their deeds, even though those deeds were already known to Allah beforehand. Decree and responsibility are not presented as contradictory alternatives to choose between.

Question 3Why does the hadith describe the moment of ensoulment as a divine secret rather than simply explaining what the soul is?

Because the nature of the soul, and the fixing of a person's ultimate destiny at that same moment, are treated as fundamentally beyond human access. The biological stages leading up to that moment, nutfah, 'alaqah, and mudghah, can be studied and described in detail, and increasingly so as embryology advances, but that physical detail does not extend to the soul itself or to why a particular destiny was recorded for a particular person, which remains a sirr ilahi (divine secret) regardless of how much is understood about the surrounding biological process.
