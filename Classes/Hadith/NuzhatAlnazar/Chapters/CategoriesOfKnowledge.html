Categories of Knowledge and Mutawatir

Recap: What Counts as Mutawatir

فَهُوَ بِاعْتِبَارِ وُصُولِهِ إِلَيْنَا: إِمَّا أَنْ يَكُونَ لَهُ طُرُقٌ بِلَا حَصْرٍ، أَوْ بِحَصْرٍ بِمَا فَوْقَ الِاثْنَيْنِ، أَوْ بِهِمَا، أَوْ بِوَاحِدٍ.
With respect to how it reaches us: it either has chains without limit, or a limited number of more than two, or exactly two, or only one.

Ibn Hajar, Nuzhat al-Nazar, on the classification of hadith by its routes.

A khabar reaches us either through numerous chains (mutawatir) or through a limited number of more than two narrators (so long as it does not fulfill the conditions of tawatur) or through exactly two or through only one. "Reaching us with two" means fewer than two is not found anywhere in the chain. If more than two narrators appear in some places along an otherwise two-narrator chain this does not harm the classification since the minimum at any point is what governs not the maximum. A report meeting the first case with its full conditions is mutawatir.

Two Categories of Knowledge

Mutawatir gives the benefit of certain knowledge ('ilm al-yaqini, sometimes also called 'ilm daruri). This is distinct from a second category, 'ilm al-nazari, controvertible or inferential knowledge. The distinction between the two explains why mutawatir occupies the epistemic position it does.

'Ilm al-Yaqini (Definitive Knowledge): yaqin is strong belief that corresponds to the truth. This is the position held by the majority; khabar mutawatir gives the benefit of definitive knowledge. This knowledge compels a person to accept it in a way that cannot be refused or refuted. It is produced directly without any process of building evidence or constructing an inference to reach it.

(1)
وَٱلْيَقِينُ هُوَ ٱلِٱعْتِقَادُ ٱلْجَازِمُ ٱلْمُطَابِقُ، وَهَذَا هُوَ ٱلْمُعْتَمَدُ؛ أَنَّ خَبَرَ ٱلتَّوَاتُرِ يُفِيدُ ٱلْعِلْمَ ٱلضَّرُورِيَّ، وَهُوَ ٱلَّذِي يُضْطَرُّ ٱلْإِنْسَانُ إِلَيْهِ بِحَيْثُ لَا يُمْكِنُهُ دَفْعُهُ.
Yaqin is the definitive belief that corresponds to the truth. This is the relied-upon position: that the report of tawatur gives the benefit of necessary knowledge, which is that which a person is compelled toward, such that he is not able to repel it.

Ibn Hajar, Nuzhat al-Nazar, on yaqin and 'ilm daruri.

'Ilm al-Nazari (Controvertible Knowledge): this is knowledge reached only through nazar (contemplation), meaning the ordering of known or presumed facts so that they lead to further facts or presumptions. Unlike yaqini knowledge, nazari knowledge does give benefit but only by way of that evidence-building process, and only to someone who actually has the capacity to reason in this way.

(2)
وَقِيلَ: لَا يُفِيدُ ٱلْعِلْمَ إِلَّا نَظَرِيّاً! وَلَيْسَ بِشَيْءٍ؛ لِأَنَّ ٱلْعِلْمَ حَاصِلٌ لِمَنْ لَيْسَتْ لَهُ أَهْلِيَّةُ ٱلنَّظَرِ، كَٱلْعَامِّيِّ، إِذِ ٱلنَّظَرُ: تَرْتِيبُ أُمُورٍ مَعْلُومَةٍ أَوْ مَظْنُونَةٍ يُتَوَصَّلُ بِهَا إِلَى عُلُومٍ أَوْ ظُنُونٍ، وَلَيْسَ فِي ٱلْعَامِّيِّ أَهْلِيَّةُ ذَلِكَ، فَلَوْ كَانَ نَظَرِيّاً لَمَا حَصَلَ لَهُمْ.
It has been said: it gives no knowledge except nazari knowledge. This is nothing, because knowledge is achieved by one who has no capacity for nazar, like the layperson, since nazar is the ordering of known or presumed matters by which one arrives at further knowledge or presumptions, and the layperson has no capacity for that. If it were nazari, it would not be achieved for them.

Ibn Hajar, Nuzhat al-Nazar, refuting the nazari position.

وَلَاحَ بِهَذَا ٱلتَّقْرِيرِ ٱلْفَرْقُ بَيْنَ ٱلْعِلْمِ ٱلضَّرُورِيِّ وَٱلْعِلْمِ ٱلنَّظَرِيِّ؛ إِذِ ٱلضَّرُورِيُّ يُفِيدُ ٱلْعِلْمَ بِلَا ٱسْتِدْلَالٍ، وَٱلنَّظَرِيُّ يُفِيدُهُ لَكِنْ مَعَ ٱلِٱسْتِدْلَالِ، وَأَنَّ ٱلضَّرُورِيَّ يَحْصُلُ لِكُلِّ سَامِعٍ، وَٱلنَّظَرِيَّ لَا يَحْصُلُ إِلَّا لِمَنْ فِيهِ أَهْلِيَّةُ ٱلنَّظَرِ.
With this account, the difference between necessary knowledge and nazari knowledge becomes clear: necessary knowledge gives its benefit without inference, while nazari knowledge gives its benefit but with inference, and necessary knowledge is achieved by every listener, while nazari knowledge is only achieved by one who has the capacity for nazar.

Ibn Hajar, Nuzhat al-Nazar, on the difference between 'ilm daruri and 'ilm nazari.

Why Mutawatir Gives Yaqini, Not Nazari, Knowledge

Some have said mutawatir gives only controvertible knowledge, not definitive knowledge. This position is rejected: knowledge by way of mutawatir is achieved even by someone with no capacity for nazar at all, such as an ordinary layperson. Nazar requires the ability to order known or presumed facts to arrive at further facts, an ability a layperson does not possess. If mutawatir produced controvertible knowledge it could never be achieved by such a person, since controvertible knowledge is only accessible to someone capable of contemplation to begin with. Since a layperson plainly does attain certainty from a mutawatir report, that certainty cannot be the nazari type. It must be yaqini, produced directly and without inference.

This is also what makes the yaqini/nazari distinction clear as a general matter beyond mutawatir specifically: definitive knowledge is available immediately to anyone who receives it regardless of their capacity for reasoning, while controvertible knowledge is gated behind the capacity to reason in the first place.

Why the Conditions of Mutawatir Are Not in the Original Text

Ibn Hajar does not list the conditions of mutawatir in the original matn of the Nukhbah itself, because mutawatir is not actually part of the discussions proper to 'ilm al-hadith. 'Ilm al-isnad exists to investigate the authenticity or weakness of a hadith so it can be acted upon or set aside based on the attributes of the narrators and the wording used in transmission. But the narrators of a mutawatir report are not investigated this way. Acting on a mutawatir report is compulsory without such investigation, which is why its conditions sit outside the main body of hadith-critical discussion even though understanding them is still necessary.

فَائِدَةٌ: ذَكَرَ ٱبْنُ ٱلصَّلَاحِ أَنَّ مِثَالَ ٱلْمُتَوَاتِرِ عَلَى ٱلتَّفْسِيرِ ٱلْمُتَقَدِّمِ يَعِزُّ وُجُودُهُ، إِلَّا أَنْ يُدَّعَى أَنَّ ذَلِكَ فِي حَدِيثِ: "مَنْ كَذَبَ عَلَيَّ مُتَعَمِّداً فَلْيَتَبَوَّأْ مَقْعَدَهُ مِنَ ٱلنَّارِ".
A benefit: Ibn al-Salah mentioned that an example of mutawatir on the aforementioned definition is rare in existence except by claiming that it applies to the hadith: "Whoever deliberately ascribes a lie to me, let him find his seat in the Fire."

Ibn Hajar, Nuzhat al-Nazar, citing Ibn al-Salah.

The Rebuttal: the claim of rarity, and the more extreme claim that no mutawatir hadith exists at all, is unfounded. Both positions stem from a lack of awareness of how extensive the chains for many reports actually are, of the true states of the narrators involved, and of the other characteristics that rule out the narrators either colluding on a lie or coincidentally narrating the same report independently.

(1)
وَمَا ٱدَّعَاهُ، نَقِيضَ دَعْوَاهُ، وَكَذَا مَا ٱدَّعَاهُ غَيْرُهُ مِنَ ٱلْعَدَمِ؛ لِأَنَّ ذَلِكَ نَشَأَ عَنْ قِلَّةِ ٱطِّلَاعٍ عَلَى كَثْرَةِ ٱلطُّرُقِ، وَأَحْوَالِ ٱلرِّجَالِ، وَصِفَاتِهِمُ ٱلْمُقْتَضِيَةِ لِإِبْعَادِ ٱلْعَادَةِ أَنْ يَتَوَاطَؤُوا عَلَى كَذِبٍ، أَوْ يَحْصُلَ مِنْهُمُ ٱتِّفَاقاً.
What he claimed is the opposite of what he claimed, and likewise what others claimed of its total absence, because that arose from a lack of awareness of the extensiveness of the chains, the states of the narrators, and their characteristics that require ruling out, by custom, that they colluded on a lie or that it occurred from them by coincidence.

Ibn Hajar, Nuzhat al-Nazar, rebutting the rarity claim.

The strongest argument for the real, significant existence of mutawatir reports rests on the famous, widespread books held by scholars across east and west, books whose ascription to their compilers is itself definitively established. When these compilers agree on recording a given hadith, and their chains are sufficient to rule out the possibility of collusion on a lie, along with the other conditions being met, this produces definitive knowledge that the report is correctly ascribed to its original speaker. Examples of this kind found across the famous books are in fact plentiful, contrary to what Ibn al-Salah and others claimed.

وَمِنْ أَحْسَنِ مَا يُقَرَّرُ بِهِ كَوْنُ ٱلْمُتَوَاتِرِ مَوْجُوداً وُجُوداً كَثِيراً: أَنَّ ٱلْكُتُبَ ٱلْمَشْهُورَةَ ٱلْمُتَدَاوَلَةَ بِأَيْدِي أَهْلِ ٱلْعِلْمِ شَرْقاً وَغَرْباً، ٱلْمَقْطُوعَ عِنْدَهُمْ بِصِحَّةِ نِسْبَتِهَا إِلَى مُصَنِّفِيهَا؛ إِذَا ٱجْتَمَعَتْ عَلَى إِخْرَاجِ حَدِيثٍ، وَتَعَدَّدَتْ طُرُقُهُ تَعَدُّداً تُحِيلُ ٱلْعَادَةُ تَوَاطُؤَهُمْ عَلَى ٱلْكَذِبِ، إِلَى آخِرِ ٱلشُّرُوطِ؛ أَفَادَ ٱلْعِلْمَ ٱلْيَقِينِيَّ بِصِحَّةِ نِسْبَتِهِ إِلَى قَائِلِهِ، وَمِثْلُ ذَلِكَ فِي ٱلْكُتُبِ ٱلْمَشْهُورَةِ كَثِيرٌ.
Among the best ways to establish that mutawatir exists abundantly is this: the famous books circulated in the hands of the people of knowledge, east and west, which are definitively known to be correctly ascribed to their compilers, when they agree on recording a hadith, and its routes multiply to a degree that custom rules out their collusion on a lie, along with the remaining conditions, this produces definitive knowledge of the correctness of its ascription to its speaker. Examples of this kind are plentiful in the famous books.

Ibn Hajar, Nuzhat al-Nazar, on the famous books as proof of mutawatir.

Question 1A layperson with no training in logic or inference hears a mutawatir report and immediately accepts it as certain, without reasoning through any evidence. A student argues this must be 'ilm al-nazari, since the person has arrived at knowledge. Is the student correct?

No. 'Ilm al-nazari can only be attained by someone capable of nazar: ordering known or presumed facts to reach further ones. A layperson lacks this capacity. Since the layperson still attains certainty that certainty must be 'ilm al-yaqini, produced directly without any evidence-building, which is exactly why mutawatir is classified as yielding yaqini knowledge rather than nazari knowledge.

Question 2Why are the conditions of mutawatir absent from the original matn of the Nukhbah, when they are clearly necessary to understand the category?

Because, strictly speaking, mutawatir does not belong to the discussions of 'ilm al-hadith proper. 'Ilm al-isnad exists to investigate the reliability of narrators and wording so a hadith can be accepted or rejected, but the narrators of a mutawatir report are never investigated this way. Acting on a mutawatir report is compulsory without investigation, so its conditions fall outside the main critical apparatus the rest of the science is built around.

Question 3Ibn al-Salah claims mutawatir examples are rare. What is the strongest argument against this claim?

The famous, widespread books of hadith, definitively known to be correctly ascribed to their compilers, frequently show multiple compilers independently agreeing on recording the same hadith, with chains sufficient to rule out collusion. This pattern, repeated across the famous books, produces definitive knowledge of correct ascription in many cases, showing that mutawatir examples are plentiful rather than rare. The rarity claim stems from underestimating the extent of the chains and the states of the narrators involved.
