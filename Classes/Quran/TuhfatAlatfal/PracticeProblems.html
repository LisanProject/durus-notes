Tuhfat al-Atfal - Practice Problems

Here are the practice problems for Tuhfat al-Atfal. Click on the "Solution" link for each problem to see the full worked solution. If you are looking for problems without solutions to test yourself cold, see the Assignment Problems page instead.

Note that some sections will have more problems than others, and problems will only appear once the corresponding notes section has been posted. Sections without problems yet are marked below.

Here is a listing of sections for which practice problems have been written, along with a brief description of what each section tests.

Chapter 1: Introduction

The Opening of the Poem: problems and solutions not yet posted

Chapter 2: Rules of the Noon Sakinah and Tanween

Idhhar Halqi: Here we identify a noon sakinah or tanween followed by one of the six throat letters and confirm clear pronunciation applies.

Idgham: Here we identify a noon sakinah or tanween followed by one of the six yarmalun letters, state whether ghunnah is retained, and watch for the single-word exception.

Iqlab: Here we identify a noon sakinah or tanween followed by ba and confirm the conversion to a nasalized meem.

Ikhfa': Here we identify a noon sakinah or tanween followed by one of the fifteen remaining letters and state which of the three degrees of ikhfa' applies.

Chapter 3: Rules of the Meem Sakinah

The Meem Sakinah: Here we identify a meem sakinah, the letter that follows it, and state which of the three rulings applies: ikhfa' shafawi, idgham shafawi, or izhar shafawi.

Chapter 4: The Lam of Al and the Lam of the Verb

Lam Qamariyyah, Lam Shamsiyyah, and the Lam of the Verb: Here we identify a lam, the context it appears in, and state which rule applies.

Chapter 5: Mithlayn, Mutajanisayn, and Mutaqaribayn

The Three Letter Relationships: Here we classify a pair of adjacent letters as mithlayn, mutajanisayn, or mutaqaribayn, and determine whether the pair is saghir or kabir.

Chapter 6: The Types of Madd

Madd Asli and Madd al-Leen: Here we distinguish a true letter of madd from a letter of leen.

Chapter 7: The Rulings of the Madd

Madd Wajib, Jaiz, and Lazim: Here we identify a letter of madd, its cause, and the ruling that governs it.

Chapter 8: The Types of Madd Lazim

Kilmi and Harfi, Mukhaffaf and Muthaqqal: Here we classify a madd lazim by its four-way type.

Chapter 9: Conclusion of the Poem

Closing Verses and the Abjad Calculation: problems and solutions not yet posted
