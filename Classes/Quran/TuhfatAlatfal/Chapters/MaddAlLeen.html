The Letters of Madd and Madd al-Leen حروف المد ومد اللين

Text
حُرُوفُهُ ثَلَاثَةٌ فَعِيهَا مِنْ لَفْظِ وَاي وَهِيَ فِي نُوحِيهَا
Vocabulary
  • حُرُوفُهُits letters; the letters of madd
  • فَعِيهَاso grasp/remember them
  • مِنْ لَفْظِ وَايْfrom the word "way"; a combination gathering waw, alif, and ya
  • فِي نُوحِيهَاan example in which all three letters of madd appear
Translation
Its letters are three, so remember them from the word "way," and they are (found) in (the example) "nuhiha."
Commentary

The three letters of madd are waw, alif, and ya, gathered here in the mnemonic "way" and demonstrated together in the single word "nuhiha," where each letter of madd appears sakin and preceded by a vowel matching it: a dammah before the waw, a kasrah before the ya, and a fatha before the alif.

Text
وَاكْسِرْ لِيَا وَضَمَّ قَبْلَ الْوَاوِ وَفَتْحٌ قَبْلَ أَلِفٍ يُلْتَزَمُ
Vocabulary
  • وَاكْسِرْ لِيَاand a kasrah before the ya
  • ضَمَّ قَبْلَ الْوَاوِa dammah before the waw
  • فَتْحٌ قَبْلَ أَلِفٍa fatha before the alif
  • يُلْتَزَمُis necessary, required
Translation
And a kasrah before the ya, and before the waw a dammah is a requirement, and a fatha before the alif is necessary.
Commentary

Each letter of madd has a strict precondition. Waw must be preceded by a dammah, ya by a kasrah, and alif always by a fatha (an alif is simply never preceded by anything else, since it is always sakin even though no sukun is written on it). A second, unstated but essential condition, visible in the example "nuhiha" itself, is that all three letters must also be sakin. A waw or ya carrying its own vowel, as in تُوَسْوِسُ, is not a letter of madd at all.

The Three Letters of Madd: alif (always preceded by fatha), waw sakinah (preceded by dammah), and ya sakinah (preceded by kasrah). All three must be sakin; a vowelled waw or ya is not a letter of madd.

(2)
Text
وَاللِّينُ مِنْهَا الْيَا وَوَاوٌ سُكِّنَا إِنِ انْفِتَاحٌ قَبْلَ كُلٍّ أُعْلِنَا
Vocabulary
  • اللِّينُleen; softness
  • سُكِّنَاthe two (waw and ya) are sakin
  • إِنِ انْفِتَاحٌif a fatha (openness)
  • أُعْلِنَاis announced, present
Translation
And leen from them (these three letters) is the ya and waw, carrying a sukun, if a fatha before each is present.
Commentary

If a waw sakinah or ya sakinah is instead preceded by a fatha rather than the dammah or kasrah required for madd, the letter is reclassified as a letter of leen (softness), not a letter of madd, e.g. the waw in خَوْفٍ or the ya in بَيْتٍ. Alif is never counted among the letters of leen, since it always follows a fatha by definition and so never meets the condition that would distinguish madd from leen in the first place.

Madd al-Leen occurs when a waw or ya is sakin and preceded by a fatha, as in خَوْفٍ (fear) or بَيْتٍ (house). It is closely related to, but distinct from, the ordinary letters of madd.
