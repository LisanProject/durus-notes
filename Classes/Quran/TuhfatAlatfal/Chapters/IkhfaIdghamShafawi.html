Ikhfa' Shafawi and Idgham Shafawi الإخفاء والإدغام الشفوي

Text
وَادِّغِمْ إِنْ تَسْكُنِ الْمِيمُ أَتَتْ بَعْدَهَا الْبَاءُ لَدَى الْأَجَلَّا
أَحْكَامُهَا ثَلَاثَةٌ لِمَنْ ضَبَطَ إِخْفَاءٌ ادْغَامٌ وَإِظْهَارٌ فَقَطْ
Vocabulary
  • وَادِّغِمْ إِنْ تَسْكُنِ الْمِيمُand the meem, when it has a sukun; the meem sakinah, a meem carrying no vowel of its own
  • أَحْكَامُهَاits rulings; referring to the meem sakinah
  • ثَلَاثَةٌthree
  • لِمَنْ ضَبَطَfor one who wants precision
  • إِخْفَاءٌ، إِدْغَامٌ، وَإِظْهَارٌikhfa', idgham, and izhar; the three rulings of the meem sakinah
  • فَقَطْonly
Translation
And the meem, when it has a sukun, coming before all the letters of the alphabet, excluding the soft alif, for the possessor of intellect. Its rulings are three for one who wants precision: ikhfa', idgham, and izhar only.
Commentary

A meem sakinah can, in principle, appear before any of the 28 letters of the alphabet except the alif, since two still letters never meet outside of a pause (waqf). Al-Jamzuri sets this up before naming the three rulings that govern which letter follows: ikhfa' shafawi (labial concealment), idgham shafawi (labial merging), and izhar shafawi (labial clarity). Each is named "shafawi," of the lips, because the meem itself is a labial letter, and the rulings that apply to it are worked out at the lips as well.

The Meem Sakinah's Three Rulings are determined entirely by the single letter that follows it, exactly as with the noon sakinah: ba triggers ikhfa' shafawi, another meem triggers idgham shafawi, and every other letter triggers izhar shafawi.

(1)

Ikhfa' Shafawi الإخفاء الشفوي

Text
فَالْأَوَّلُ الْإِخْفَاءُ عِنْدَ الْبَاءِ وَسَمِّهِ الشَّفَوِيَّ لِلْقُرَّاءِ
Vocabulary
  • فَالْأَوَّلُso the first (of the three rulings)
  • الْإِخْفَاءُikhfa'; concealment
  • عِنْدَ الْبَاءِwhen the ba follows
  • وَسَمِّهِ الشَّفَوِيَّand name it labial
  • لِلْقُرَّاءِaccording to all the reciters
Translation
So the first (ruling) is ikhfa', when the ba follows. And name it (this ikhfa') labial, according to all the reciters.
Commentary

When a meem sakinah is immediately followed by ba, ikhfa' shafawi applies: the meem is concealed with a light nasal sound, the lips coming together softly rather than closing completely, since a full closure would collapse the concealment into idgham. This ruling is agreed upon by every school of recitation, unlike some of the finer points elsewhere in Tajwid where reciters differ.

ExampleIdentify the meem sakinah and confirm ikhfa' shafawi applies.تَرْمِيهِم بِحِجَارَةٍ

"striking them with stones"

The meem sakinah in تَرْمِيهِمْ is immediately followed by ba in بِحِجَارَةٍ. Ikhfa' shafawi applies: the meem is concealed with a light nasal sound rather than pronounced clearly or fully merged.

Idgham Shafawi الإدغام الشفوي

Text
وَالثَّانِي إِدْغَامٌ بِمِثْلِهَا أَتَى وَسَمِّ إِدْغَامًا صَغِيرًا يَا فَتَى
Vocabulary
  • وَالثَّانِيand the second (ruling)
  • إِدْغَامٌ بِمِثْلِهَاidgham with its equivalent, i.e. another meem
  • إِدْغَامًا صَغِيرًاidgham saghir; minor idgham
  • يَا فَتَىoh young student; a direct address to the learner
Translation
And the second (ruling) is idgham, with its equivalent appearing. And name it idgham saghir, oh student.
Commentary

When a meem sakinah is followed by another meem, the first meem merges fully into the second, and the two are read as a single meem carrying a shaddah and full ghunnah. This is called idgham saghir (minor merging) because the first letter is still (sakin) and the second is vowelled, the same "small" pattern seen with mithlayn elsewhere in the poem.

ExampleIdentify the meem sakinah and confirm idgham shafawi applies.لَكُمْ مَا فِي الْأَرْضِ

"for you is whatever is on the earth"

The meem sakinah in لَكُمْ is immediately followed by another meem in مَا. Idgham shafawi applies: the two meems merge into a single, fully nasalized, elongated meem.

Example phrases are illustrative rather than citations of specific ayat; always verify against the mushaf and a qualified reciter.
