Iqlab الإقلاب

Text
وَالثَّالِثُ الْإِقْلَابُ عِنْدَ الْبَاءِ *** مِيْمًا بِغُنَّةٍ مَعَ الْإِخْفَاءِ
Vocabulary
  • الْإِقْلَابconversion; changing the noon sakinah or tanween into a different sound entirely
  • عِنْدَ الْبَاءِwhen the letter ba follows
  • مِيْمًا(converted) into a meem
  • بِغُنَّةٍwith nasalization
  • مَعَ الْإِخْفَاءِalong with concealment; the converted meem is itself lightly concealed, not fully closed
Translation
The third (ruling) is iqlab, when ba follows: (the noon or tanween is converted) into a meem, with nasalization, along with concealment.
Commentary

Iqlab is the smallest of the four rulings in scope, triggered by a single letter, ba, but it is unique in kind: rather than pronouncing the noon clearly (idhhar) or merging it into the next letter unchanged (idgham), the reciter actually replaces the noon sound with a meem sound before pronouncing ba.

Iqlab converts a noon sakinah or tanween into a hidden, nasalized meem sound whenever it is immediately followed by the letter ba. The lips come together lightly, as if forming a meem, but the closure is soft rather than complete, which is why the poem describes it as carrying ikhfa' (concealment) as well as ghunnah.

(4)

Two closure techniques are found among reciters. Some close the lips fully before releasing into the ba, while others, following the style of reciters like al-Minshawi, al-Husary, and Abdul-Basit, leave a slight gap between the lips, which softens the sound and avoids harshness. Either way, the underlying letter written on the page remains noon or tanween; only the sound produced changes, which is why this ruling is called "conversion" rather than "merging."

Here are worked examples of iqlab in each of the contexts where it occurs: noon sakinah, tanween, and within a single word.

Example 1 (Noon sakinah)Identify the noon sakinah and explain what happens to it here.مِنْ بَعْدِ

"after" / "from after"

The noon sakinah in مِنْ is immediately followed by ba in بَعْدِ. Iqlab applies: the noon sound is converted into a light, nasalized meem before the ba is pronounced.

Example 2 (Tanween)Identify the tanween and explain what happens to it here.سَمِيعٌ بَصِيرٌ

"All-Hearing, All-Seeing"

The tanween at the end of سَمِيعٌ is immediately followed by ba in بَصِيرٌ. Iqlab applies to tanween exactly as it does to a written noon sakinah: the added n sound is converted into a nasalized meem before moving into the ba.

Example 3 (Within a single word)Identify the noon sakinah and explain what happens to it here.أَنبِئْهُم

"inform them"

Here the noon sakinah and the ba occur inside a single word rather than across two separate words. Unlike idgham, iqlab has no "single word" exception: it applies exactly the same way whether the noon and ba are in one word or two, so the noon still converts to a nasalized meem before the ba.

Example phrases above are illustrative of iqlab rather than citations of specific ayat; always verify wording and application against the mushaf and a qualified reciter.
