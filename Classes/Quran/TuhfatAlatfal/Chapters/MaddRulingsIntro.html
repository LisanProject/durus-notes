Chapter 7: The Rulings of the Madd

With the basic categories of madd established in the previous chapter, this chapter covers the three rulings that attach to madd far'i: wujub (compulsory), jawaz (permissible), and luzum (necessary). Each specific type of madd far'i, muttasil, munfasil, 'arid li-l-sukun, and badal, falls under one of these three rulings, and the ruling determines how much freedom a reciter has in how long to pull it. This chapter assumes the student is already comfortable with what each named madd is; the focus here is strictly on the ruling attached to each.

Here is a list of the sections covered in this chapter.

Madd Wajib and Madd Jaiz: Madd muttasil, which is compulsory, and the three jaiz madds, munfasil, 'arid li-l-sukun, and badal, where either pulling or shortening is permitted.

Madd Lazim: The necessary madd, triggered by a permanent sukun, and why it is always pulled to its full six-count duration.
