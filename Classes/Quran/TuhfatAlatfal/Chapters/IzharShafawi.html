Izhar Shafawi الإظهار الشفوي

Text
وَالثَّالِثُ الْإِظْهَارُ فِي الْبَقِيَّةِ مِنْ أَحْرُفٍ وَسَمِّهَا شَفَوِيَّةْ
Vocabulary
  • وَالثَّالِثُand the third (ruling)
  • الْإِظْهَارُizhar; clear pronunciation
  • فِي الْبَقِيَّةِ مِنْ أَحْرُفٍin the remaining letters
  • وَسَمِّهَا شَفَوِيَّةْand name it (this izhar) labial
Translation
And the third (ruling) is izhar, in the remaining letters. And name it (this izhar) labial.
Commentary

Once ba (ikhfa' shafawi) and meem (idgham shafawi) are accounted for, every other letter of the alphabet triggers izhar shafawi: the meem sakinah is pronounced clearly, with no nasal blending into what follows. It is called labial for the same reason as the other two rulings, since the meem itself is articulated at the lips.

Izhar Shafawi is the default outcome for the meem sakinah, covering every letter of the alphabet except ba and meem. The meem is released clearly with no extra nasal pull, exactly as izhar halqi is the default for the noon sakinah before the six throat letters.

Text
وَاحْذَرْ لَدَى وَاوٍ وَفَا أَنْ تَخْتَفِي لِقُرْبِهَا وَالِاتِّحَادِ فَاعْرِفِ
Vocabulary
  • وَاحْذَرْbeware, be careful
  • لَدَى وَاوٍ وَفَاat a waw and a fa
  • أَنْ تَخْتَفِيthat it (the meem) be hidden
  • لِقُرْبِهَاdue to its nearness (to the fa in makhraj)
  • وَالِاتِّحَادِand unity (in makhraj with the waw)
  • فَاعْرِفِso know this
Translation
Be careful at a waw and a fa, that it (the meem) be hidden due to its nearness (to the fa in makhraj) and unity (in makhraj with the waw), so know this.
Commentary

This closing caution addresses a specific pronunciation hazard rather than a new ruling. Fa and meem are articulated close to one another, and waw shares its makhraj with meem entirely (both are pronounced by rounding the lips). Because of this closeness, a reciter can easily let izhar shafawi collapse into something that sounds concealed when the meem is followed by either of these two letters, even though the correct ruling remains plain izhar. Al-Jamzuri's point is purely one of care in execution, not a fourth ruling: the student must consciously keep the meem clear precisely because these two letters make that clarity harder to produce than it looks.

Here are worked examples of izhar shafawi, including the fa/waw caution.

Example 1 (ordinary izhar shafawi)Identify the meem sakinah and confirm izhar shafawi applies.أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ

"You have favored them"

The meem sakinah in عَلَيْهِمْ is followed by nothing in this phrase (it ends the ayah), but taking it in context with a following word beginning in a letter other than ba or meem, izhar shafawi is the default: the meem is pronounced clearly with full closure and release of the lips.

Example 2 (the waw caution)Identify the meem sakinah and explain why extra care is needed here.هُمْ وَلَا

"they, nor..."

The meem sakinah in هُمْ is followed by waw in وَلَا. The ruling is still izhar shafawi, but because waw shares the meem's own makhraj (both are formed with rounded, closed lips), a reciter must take extra care to release the meem cleanly rather than letting it blur toward the waw.
