Lam Qamariyyah اللام القمرية

Text
لِلَامِ أَلْ حَالَانِ قَبْلَ أَحْرُفِ الْأُولَى إِظْهَارُهَا فَلْتَعْرِفِ
قَبْلَ أَرْبَعٍ مَعْ عَشَرَةٍ خُذْ عِلْمَهُ مِنِ ابْغِ حَجَّكَ وَخَفْ عَقِيمَهُ
Vocabulary
  • لِلَامِ أَلْfor the lam of "al"; the definite article
  • حَالَانِtwo conditions
  • الْأُولَىthe first (of the two)
  • إِظْهَارُهَاits izhar; the clear pronunciation of the lam
  • قَبْلَ أَرْبَعٍ مَعْ عَشَرَةٍbefore four with ten, i.e. fourteen letters
  • خُذْ عِلْمَهُtake its knowledge (of the fourteen letters)
Translation
For the lam of "al" there are two conditions before the letters (of the alphabet). The first of the two is its izhar, so know this. Before fourteen (letters); take its knowledge from (the words): "Be desirous of your pilgrimage and fear that it be barren."
Commentary

Lam al-ta'rif, the lam of the definite article "al," cannot be void of one of two outcomes depending on the letter that follows it. This section covers the first: izhar qamari, clear pronunciation, which applies before fourteen specific letters. Al-Jamzuri packs all fourteen into the initial letters of the memorable phrase "ibghi hajjaka wakhaf 'aqimahu" (be desirous of your pilgrimage and fear that it be barren), a device identical in spirit to the mnemonic phrases already used for the letters of ikhfa' and idgham earlier in the poem.

Lam Qamariyyah is named for the word "al-qamar" (the moon), itself one of the fourteen letters covered by this rule: the lam is pronounced clearly (izhar) whenever it is followed by a hamzah, ba, jeem, ha, kha, 'ayn, ghayn, fa, qaf, kaf, meem, waw, ha, or ya.

(1)

Here are worked examples of lam qamariyyah.

Example 1Identify the letter after the lam of "al" and confirm izhar qamari applies.الْقَمَرِ

"the moon"

The lam of "al" is followed by qaf, one of the fourteen qamari letters (and the very word the rule is named after). The lam is pronounced clearly, with no assimilation.

Example 2Identify the letter after the lam of "al" and confirm izhar qamari applies.الْحَمْدُ

"the praise"

The lam of "al" is followed by ha, one of the fourteen qamari letters. The lam is pronounced clearly, exactly as it is written.
