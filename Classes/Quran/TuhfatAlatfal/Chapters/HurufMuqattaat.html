The Huruf Muqatta'at at the Openings of Surahs فواتح السور

Text
وَجَائِزٌ مَدُّ وَقَصْرٌ إِنْ عَرَضْ سُكُونُ وَقْفٍ بَعْدَ حَرْفٍ قَدِ انْحَصَرْ
وَذَاكَ أَيْضًا فِي فَوَاتِحِ السُّوَرْ فِي لَفْظِ حَيٍّ طَاهِرٍ قَدِ انْحَصَرْ
Vocabulary
  • فَوَاتِحِ السُّوَرْthe openings of the surahs
  • حَيٍّ طَاهِرٍa pure and clean life; a mnemonic gathering five specific letters
  • قَدِ انْحَصَرْverily confined, limited to
Translation
And those (letters) are also at the beginning of the surahs. In the expression "hayyun tahirun" they are confined.
Commentary

The Qur'an opens 29 surahs with isolated letters, the huruf muqatta'at. Not every letter in these openings behaves the same way. Al-Jamzuri narrows in on five specific letters, gathered in the mnemonic "hayyun tahirun" (ha, ya, ta, ha, ra), where madd tabi'i (the ordinary two-count madd) applies, since the middle letter of each of these letter-names is a letter of madd but nothing follows it that would trigger madd lazim: ha (in ha-meem), ya (in ya-sin and ta-ha), ta (in ta-ha, ta-sin-meem, ta-sin), ha again, and ra (in alif-lam-ra and alif-lam-meem-ra).

Madd Tabi'i Harfi: five letters among the huruf muqatta'at, ha, ya, ta, ha, and ra, take only the ordinary two-count madd, since nothing in their spelled form triggers a lazim-level sukun.

Text
يَجْمَعُهَا حُرُوفُ كَمْ عَسَلْ نَقَصْ وَعَيْنُ ذُو وَجْهَيْنِ وَالطُّولُ أَخَصّ
Vocabulary
  • يَجْمَعُهَاgathers them (the eight letters)
  • كَمْ عَسَلْ نَقَصْ"how much of the honey diminished?"; a mnemonic gathering eight letters
  • ذُو وَجْهَيْنِholder of two options/ways
  • وَالطُّولُ أَخَصّwhile tul is preferred
Translation
The letters "kam 'asal naqas" gathers them (the eight letters), and the 'ayn has two options, while tul is preferred.
Commentary

Eight letters among the huruf muqatta'at, kaf, meem, 'ayn, seen, lam, noon, qaf, and sad, always take madd lazim harfi, gathered in the mnemonic "kam 'asal naqas." Each of these letter-names, when spelled out, has a madd letter in the middle followed by a genuine permanent sukun (produced by a following letter that is either doubled or simply sakin in the spelled-out name itself), qualifying as madd lazim rather than madd tabi'i.

The 'ayn is singled out as carrying two valid options rather than a single fixed duration: tawassut (medium, 4-5 harakat) or tul (full, 6 harakat), with tul preferred among the majority of reciters. Some reciters describe the two options differently, as qasr and tawassut with tul still preferred as an additional allowance, but either way, tul remains the recommended choice for the 'ayn specifically. Every other letter in this list of eight is pulled to tul without any such flexibility.

Text
وَمَا سِوَى الْحَرْفِ الثُّلَاثِيِّ لَا أَلِفْ فَمَدُّهُ مَدًّا طَبِيعِيًّا أُلِفْ
وَذَاكَ أَيْضًا فِي فَوَاتِحِ السُّوَرْ حُرُوفٌ الِسِّتَّةُ عَشَرَ قَدِ انْحَصَرْ
يَجْمَعُ الْفَوَاتِحَ أَرْبَعَ عَشَرَ صِلْ سُحَيْرًا مِنْ قَطَعْكَ ذَا اشْتَهَرْ
Vocabulary
  • وَمَا سِوَىand all besides
  • الْحَرْفِ الثُّلَاثِيِّthe tripled letters
  • لَا أَلِفْnot the alif; excluding the alif
  • صِلْ سُحَيْرًا مِنْ قَطَعْكَa mnemonic gathering all fourteen distinct letters of the huruf muqatta'at
Translation
And all besides the tripled letters, excluding the alif, then its madd is known as madd tabi'i. The letters "sil suhayran min qat'ika" gather the fourteen distinct openers; this is well known.
Commentary

Combining the previous two sections, the huruf muqatta'at divide into four total behaviors: the eight letters of "kam 'asal naqas" take madd lazim (mostly tul, with the 'ayn's two options); the five letters of "hayyun tahirun" take ordinary madd tabi'i; the alif takes no madd whatsoever, since spelling it out does not produce a middle letter of madd at all; and across all the surah openings combined, only fourteen distinct letters ever appear, gathered in the phrase "sil suhayran min qat'ika" (join ties in the morning with him who has cut ties with you).

The Alif's Exception: alif is the only letter among the huruf muqatta'at that receives no madd at all when spelled out, since its own name does not contain a letter of madd in the relevant position, unlike the other letters covered in this chapter.
