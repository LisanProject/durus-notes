Chapter 5: Mithlayn, Mutajanisayn, and Mutaqaribayn

This chapter steps back from specific letters (noon, meem, lam) to the general question of how any two adjacent letters relate to each other, based on their makharij (articulation points) and sifat (characteristics). Closeness between two letters has three recognized degrees, from strongest to weakest: mithlayn (identical letters), mutajanisayn (same makhraj, different sifat), and mutaqaribayn (close makhraj, different sifat). This chapter assumes familiarity with makharij and sifat from prior study; a student who has not yet covered those topics will find this chapter difficult to follow without that background.

Here is a list of the sections covered in this chapter.

The Three Letter Relationships: The definitions of mithlayn, mutajanisayn, and mutaqaribayn, with real examples of each.

Saghir and Kabir: How each of the three relationships further splits into a "small" and "large" form depending on whether the first letter is still or vowelled, determining whether idgham is made.
