Chapter 3: Rules of the Meem Sakinah

Before turning to the meem sakinah itself, al-Jamzuri closes out the previous chapter with a short rule on the mushaddad noon and meem: whenever either carries a shaddah, ghunnah must be made clearly, and each is called a "letter of ghunnah." With that settled, this chapter covers the meem sakinah, a meem carrying no vowel. Exactly as with the noon sakinah, everything comes down to the single letter that follows it: three outcomes are possible, each triggered by a specific set of letters, and all three are variations on the labial articulation of the meem itself.

Here is a list of the sections covered in this chapter.

Ikhfa' Shafawi and Idgham Shafawi: What happens when a meem sakinah meets a ba (concealment) or another meem (merging), the two rules that share the meem's own labial point of articulation.

Izhar Shafawi: The default clear pronunciation that applies for all the remaining letters, and the special care needed before waw and fa, where the meem's closeness to both can blur the clarity.
