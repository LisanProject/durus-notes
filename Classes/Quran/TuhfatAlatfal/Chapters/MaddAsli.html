Madd Asli (Tabi'i) المد الأصلي (الطبيعي)

Text
وَالْمَدُّ أَصْلِيٌّ وَفَرْعِيٌّ لَهُ وَسَمِّ أَوَّلًا طَبِيعِيًّا وَهُوَ
Vocabulary
  • الْمَدُّthe pulling, extending, lengthening (of sound)
  • أَصْلِيٌّprimary
  • فَرْعِيٌّsecondary
  • طَبِيعِيًّاnatural
Translation
And the madd is (either) primary or secondary to it, and name the first a natural (madd), and it is...
Commentary

Madd literally means to lengthen or extend; technically, it means prolonging the sound at a letter of madd or a letter of leen. Al-Jamzuri opens this chapter by splitting madd into two broad categories: asli (primary), also called tabi'i (natural), and far'i (secondary). A person with an ordinary, natural disposition will lengthen madd tabi'i for exactly its standard duration without any deficiency or exaggeration, which is why it carries that name.

Madd Tabi'i (Natural Madd) is pulled for the duration of one alif, equal to two harakat (a harakah being the time it takes to open or close a finger). It requires no external cause and is simply a property of the letter of madd itself.

Text
مَا لَا تَوَقُّفٌ لَهُ عَلَى سَبَبْ وَلَا بِدُونِهِ الْحُرُوفُ تُجْتَلَبْ
Vocabulary
  • مَاthat which
  • لَا تَوَقُّفٌdoes not depend
  • سَبَبْcause
  • بِدُونِهِwithout it (the madd-pull)
  • تُجْتَلَبْcannot be brought forth, cannot be read
Translation
That which has no dependence on a cause, and without it (the madd-pull), these letters cannot be brought forth (cannot be read).
Commentary

Madd tabi'i does not depend on any external cause the way madd far'i will. In fact, the letters of madd cannot even be pronounced correctly without some pull: if the alif in قَالَ were not lengthened at all, the word would collapse into something closer to قَلَ. The same holds for the ya in قِيلَ and the waw in يَقُولُ: each requires its minimum pull simply to remain the letter it is.

Text
بَلْ أَيُّ حَرْفٍ غَيْرَ هَمْزٍ أَوْ سُكُونْ جَا بَعْدَ مَدٍّ فَالطَّبِيعِيُّ يَكُونْ
Vocabulary
  • بَلْbut, in fact
  • أَيُّ حَرْفٍwhichever letter
  • غَيْرَ هَمْزٍ أَوْ سُكُونْbesides a hamzah or sukun
  • جَا بَعْدَ مَدٍّcomes after a letter of madd
Translation
In fact, (if) any letter besides the hamzah or sukun appears after a (letter of) madd, then the natural (madd) results.
Commentary

Here al-Jamzuri names the two causes that would upgrade a madd from tabi'i to far'i: a hamzah, or a sukun. As long as neither follows a letter of madd, the madd remains simple tabi'i, pulled for its standard two harakat and no more.

Text
وَالْآخَرُ الْفَرْعِيُّ مَوْقُوفٌ عَلَى سَبَبٍ كَهَمْزٍ أَوْ سُكُونٍ مُسْجَلَا
Vocabulary
  • الْآخَرُthe other (madd)
  • مَوْقُوفٌdependent
  • كَهَمْزٍ أَوْ سُكُونٍlike a hamzah or a sukun
  • مُسْجَلَاin general, wherever it occurs
Translation
And the other (madd) is the secondary, dependent on a reason like a hamzah or sukun in general.
Commentary

Madd far'i is that madd which is pulled longer than madd tabi'i precisely because a cause is present: a hamzah or a sukun follows the letter of madd. Which specific type of madd far'i results, and for how long it is pulled, depends on the details covered in the next two chapters: whether the hamzah is in the same word as the madd letter or the next word, and whether the sukun is permanent or only appears at a pause.

Here are worked examples of madd tabi'i across all three letters of madd.

Example 1 (Alif)Identify the letter of madd and confirm it is madd tabi'i.قَالَ

"he said"

The alif in قَالَ is preceded by a fatha, as every alif must be. Nothing follows it besides the end of the word, no hamzah, no sukun, so this is plain madd tabi'i, pulled for two harakat only.

Example 2 (Waw)Identify the letter of madd and confirm it is madd tabi'i.يَقُولُ

"he says"

The waw in يَقُولُ is sakin and preceded by a dammah, satisfying the waw's condition for being a letter of madd. Followed only by the final dammah of the word, with no hamzah or sukun, this is madd tabi'i.

Example 3 (Ya)Identify the letter of madd and confirm it is madd tabi'i.قِيلَ

"it is said"

The ya in قِيلَ is sakin and preceded by a kasrah, satisfying the ya's condition for being a letter of madd. Nothing but an ordinary letter follows it, so this too is madd tabi'i.
