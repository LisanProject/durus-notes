The Three Letter Relationships المتماثلان والمتجانسان والمتقاربان

Text
فَادَّغِمَنْ فِيهِمَا إِنْ فِي الصِّفَاتِ وَالْمَخَارِجِ اتَّفَقْ حَرْفَانِ
Vocabulary
  • حَرْفَانِtwo letters
  • اتَّفَقْagree
  • فِي الصِّفَاتِ وَالْمَخَارِجِin characteristics and articulation points
  • فَالْمِثْلَانِthen (the two letters) will be mithlayn
Translation
If in sifat and makharij two letters agree, then it is more befitting that they be mithlayn.
Commentary

Mithlayn (also called mutamathilayn) is the strongest of the three relationships: two letters that share both makhraj and sifat completely, meaning they are the same letter twice. Two meems, two bas, or two of any identical letter appearing consecutively are mithlayn.

Mithlayn: two letters identical in both makhraj and sifat, i.e. the same letter repeated. Example: the two meems in لَكُمْ مَا, or the two bas in اضْرِبْ بِعَصَاكَ.

(1)
Text
وَإِنْ يَكُونَا مَخْرَجًا تَقَارَبَا وَفِي الصِّفَاتِ اخْتَلَفَا يُلَقَّبَا مُتَقَارِبَيْنِ
Vocabulary
  • يَكُونَاif they (the two letters) are
  • مَخْرَجًا تَقَارَبَاclose in makhraj
  • اخْتَلَفَاthey differ
  • يُلَقَّبَاthey are named/called
Translation
And if they are close in makhraj, and in sifat they differ, then they are called mutaqaribayn.
Commentary

Mutaqaribayn is the weakest of the three relationships: the two letters come from articulation points that are near each other but not identical, and their sifat also differ. Lam and ra are a classic example, since both are pronounced close to the front of the tongue but ra carries takrir (a slight trill) that lam does not.

Mutaqaribayn: two letters close in makhraj, differing in sifat. Example: lam and ra in قُلْ رَبِّ, or qaf and kaf in أَلَمْ نَخْلُقكُّمْ.

(2)
Text
مُتَقَارِبَيْنِ أَوْ يَكُونَا اتَّفَقَا فِي مَخْرَجٍ دُونَ الصِّفَاتِ حُقِّقَا بِالْمُتَجَانِسَيْنِ
Vocabulary
  • اتَّفَقَاthey (the two letters) agree
  • دُونَ الصِّفَاتِbut not (in) sifat
  • حُقِّقَاthey are confirmed
  • بِالْمُتَجَانِسَيْنِas mutajanisayn (homogeneous)
Translation
Or they agree in makhraj but not in sifat, then they are confirmed as mutajanisayn.
Commentary

Mutajanisayn sits between the other two in strength: the two letters share the exact same makhraj, but their sifat differ. Ta and tta (emphatic) come from the same point on the tongue but differ in several characteristics, including whether the tongue rises toward the palate.

Mutajanisayn: two letters sharing the same makhraj, differing in sifat. Example: ta and tta in أَثْقَلَت دَّعَوَا, or ba and meem in ارْكَب مَّعَنَا.

(3)

Taken together, these three relationships rank letters by closeness: mithlayn (identical) is the strongest cause for idgham, mutajanisayn (same makhraj) is next, and mutaqaribayn (close makhraj) is the weakest of the three genuine causes. Any two letters that fit none of these three categories are simply "distant" (mutaba'idan) from each other and are read with izhar, a default so obvious that the poem does not even bother stating it explicitly.

Here are worked examples of each relationship, to practice telling them apart.

Example 1 (Mithlayn)Identify the two adjacent letters and classify their relationship.إِذْ ذَهَبَ

"when he went"

The dhal at the end of إِذْ meets the dhal at the start of ذَهَبَ. Since both letters are identical in makhraj and sifat, this is mithlayn.

Example 2 (Mutajanisayn)Identify the two adjacent letters and classify their relationship.قَالَتْ طَائِفَةٌ

"a group said"

The ta at the end of قَالَتْ meets the tta (emphatic ta) at the start of طَائِفَةٌ. Both share the same makhraj (the tip of the tongue against the base of the upper front teeth) but differ in sifat, since tta carries isti'la and itbaq which ta does not. This is mutajanisayn.

Example 3 (Mutaqaribayn)Identify the two adjacent letters and classify their relationship.قُلْ رَبِّ

"Say: my Lord"

The lam at the end of قُلْ meets the ra at the start of رَبِّ. Lam and ra are close in makhraj (both near the tip of the tongue) but differ in sifat, since ra carries takrir which lam does not. This is mutaqaribayn, the weakest of the three relationships.
