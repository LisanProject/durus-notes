Lam Shamsiyyah اللام الشمسية

Text
ثَانِيهِمَا إِدْغَامُهَا فِي أَرْبَعٍ وَعَشَرَةٍ أَيْضًا وَرَمْزَهَا فَعِ
طِبْ ثُمَّ صِلْ رَحْمًا تَفُزْ ضِفْ ذَا نِعَمْ دَعْ سُوءَ ظَنٍّ زُرْ شَرِيفًا لِلْكَرَمْ
وَاللَّامُ الْأُولَى سَمِّهَا قَمَرِيَّهْ وَاللَّامُ الْأُخْرَى سَمِّهَا شَمْسِيَّهْ
Vocabulary
  • ثَانِيهِمَاthe second of the two (conditions)
  • إِدْغَامُهَاidgham of it; the lam of "al" merging into the following letter
  • فِي أَرْبَعٍ وَعَشَرَةٍin four and ten, i.e. fourteen (letters)
  • وَرَمْزَهَا فَعِand its combination, so memorize (it)
  • قَمَرِيَّهْ / شَمْسِيَّهْlunar / solar; the names given to the two lams based on al-qamar and al-shams
Translation
The second of the two (conditions) is idgham of it (the lam of "al"), in fourteen (letters) also, so memorize its combination: "Be good, then join family ties and you will be successful. Be hospitable to the possessor of bounties. Shun evil thought. Visit the distinguished for generosity." And the first lam, name it qamariyyah, and the second lam, name it shamsiyyah.
Commentary

The second outcome for lam al-ta'rif is idgham: the lam is not pronounced at all, and the following letter is instead doubled with a shaddah. This happens before the other fourteen letters of the alphabet, gathered here in a second mnemonic phrase whose initial letters spell out the full set, mirroring the qamariyyah phrase from the previous section.

Lam Shamsiyyah is named for the word "al-shams" (the sun): in "ash-shams," the lam is not pronounced, and the shin that follows is doubled instead. It applies before ta, tha, dal, dhal, ra, zay, seen, sheen, sad, dad, ta (emphatic), za, lam, and noon.

(2)

Al-Jamzuri names the two lams directly after explaining both rulings: the lam read with izhar is qamariyyah (lunar), since "al-qamar" itself demonstrates it, and the lam read with idgham is shamsiyyah (solar), since "ash-shams" demonstrates it. This naming convention, deriving the rule's name from a word that itself contains the example, is a small but memorable teaching device worth noticing.

Here are worked examples of lam shamsiyyah.

Example 1Identify the letter after the lam of "al" and confirm idgham shamsi applies.الشَّمْسِ

"the sun"

The lam of "al" is followed by sheen, one of the fourteen shamsi letters (and the very word the rule is named after). The lam is not pronounced; the sheen is doubled instead.

Example 2Identify the letter after the lam of "al" and confirm idgham shamsi applies.الرَّحْمَنِ

"the Most Merciful"

The lam of "al" is followed by ra, one of the fourteen shamsi letters. The lam is not pronounced; the ra is doubled instead, producing the familiar "ar-Rahman."
