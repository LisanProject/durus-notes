Madd Lazim المد اللازم

Text
وَلَازِمٌ إِنِ السُّكُونُ أُصِّلَا وَصْلًا وَوَقْفًا بَعْدَ مَدٍّ طُوِّلَا
Vocabulary
  • لَازِمٌnecessary
  • أُصِّلَاregarded as original/permanent
  • وَصْلًا وَوَقْفًاduring waqf and wasl (stopping and joining)
  • طُوِّلَاlengthened to the full duration of tul
Translation
And (madd will be) lazim if the sukun is permanent, (present) during waqf and wasl, after the letter of madd, tul is made (in it).
Commentary

Madd lazim (the necessary madd) is the fourth and final type of madd far'i, and the only one carrying the luzum ruling rather than wujub or jawaz. It occurs when a letter of madd is followed by a permanent sukun, one that stays sakin whether the reciter continues (wasl) or pauses (waqf), unlike the temporary sukun behind madd 'arid. Since the ruling is lazim, only one duration is permitted: tul, the full six harakat (equal to three alifs), with no shortening allowed at all.

Madd Lazim: a letter of madd followed by a permanent sukun, one that remains sakin in both wasl and waqf. Always pulled the full six harakat (tul), with no permitted variation.

(3)

The next chapter breaks madd lazim down further into its four specific subtypes, kilmi and harfi, each further split into mukhaffaf and muthaqqal, since simply knowing "this is madd lazim" does not yet tell a reciter which specific word-pattern or huruf muqatta'at situation is being read.

Here is a worked example of madd lazim.

ExampleIdentify the letter of madd, the permanent sukun, and confirm the duration required.وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ

"nor of those who have gone astray"

The alif in الضَّالِّينَ is followed by a doubled (mushaddad) lam, which represents a sukun on the first lam followed by a vowelled lam. That sukun is permanent, present in both wasl and waqf. Madd lazim applies, requiring the full six-harakat tul.
