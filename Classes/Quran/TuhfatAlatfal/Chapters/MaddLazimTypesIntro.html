Chapter 8: The Types of Madd Lazim

Having established that madd lazim always means a full, fixed six-harakat pull, this chapter breaks the category down into its four subtypes. Madd lazim is first split into kilmi (occurring within an ordinary word) and harfi (occurring within the huruf muqatta'at, the "cut letters" that open certain surahs), and each of those is further split into muthaqqal (heavy, when idgham follows) and mukhaffaf (light, when it does not). The second half of the chapter works through the huruf muqatta'at letter by letter, since they carry their own dedicated rules that come up nowhere else in the poem.

Here is a list of the sections covered in this chapter.

Kilmi and Harfi, Mukhaffaf and Muthaqqal: The four-way classification of madd lazim, with examples of each combination.

The Huruf Muqatta'at at the Openings of Surahs: The letters that open certain surahs, how many carry madd lazim, and the special two-option ruling given to the letter 'ayn.
