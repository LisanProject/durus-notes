Madd Wajib and Madd Jaiz المد الواجب والجائز

Text
لِلْمَدِّ أَحْكَامٌ ثَلَاثَةٌ تَدُومْ وَهْيَ الْوُجُوبُ وَالْجَوَازُ وَاللُّزُومْ
Vocabulary
  • أَحْكَامٌrulings
  • تَدُومْalways, constantly
  • الْوُجُوبُwujub; compulsory
  • الْجَوَازُjawaz; permissible
  • اللُّزُومْluzum; necessary
Translation
For the madd there are always three rulings: they are wujub, jawaz, and luzum.
Commentary

Three rulings attach to the different types of madd far'i: those that are wajib (compulsory to pull), those that are jaiz (permissible, meaning either pulling or shortening is allowed), and those that are lazim (necessary, meaning a full, fixed pull is required).

Text
فَوَاجِبٌ إِنْ جَاءَ هَمْزٌ بَعْدَ مَدّ فِي كَلِمَةٍ وَذَا بِمُتَّصِلْ يُعَدّ
Vocabulary
  • فَوَاجِبٌso wajib, compulsory
  • هَمْزٌ بَعْدَ مَدّa hamzah after a letter of madd
  • فِي كَلِمَةٍin one word
  • بِمُتَّصِلْ يُعَدّit is counted as muttasil (the joined madd)
Translation
So (it is) compulsory if a hamzah appears after a (letter of) madd in one word. And this (madd) is counted as muttasil.
Commentary

Madd muttasil (the joined madd) occurs when the letter of madd is followed by a hamzah within the same word, as in جَاءَ or السَّمَاءِ. Its ruling is wajib according to every school of recitation, though reciters differ on exactly how many harakat: in the narration of Hafs via the Shatibiyyah, it is pulled 4 or 5 harakat.

Madd Muttasil (Wajib): a letter of madd followed by a hamzah within the same word. Pulled 4 to 5 harakat, compulsory according to all reciters.

Text
وَجَائِزٌ مَدٌّ وَقَصْرٌ إِنْ فُصِلْ كُلٌّ بِكَلِمَةٍ وَهَذَا الْمُنْفَصِلْ
Vocabulary
  • جَائِزٌpermissible, allowed
  • مَدٌّ وَقَصْرٌpull or shorten
  • فُصِلْseparate
  • الْمُنْفَصِلْmunfasil; the separated madd
Translation
And it is jaiz: to pull or to shorten, if each (the letter of madd and the hamzah) is separate in a word of their own. And this (madd) is munfasil.
Commentary

Madd munfasil (the separated madd) is the first of three types falling under the jaiz ruling. Here, the letter of madd ends one word and the hamzah begins the next, as in يَا أَيُّهَا or بِمَا أُنزِلَ. Since the ruling is jaiz, a reciter may pull it 4 or 5 harakat (via the Shatibiyyah), or shorten it to 2 harakat (a shortening allowed via the Tayyibah).

Text
وَمِثْلُ ذَا إِنْ عَرَضَ السُّكُونُ وَقْفًا كَتَعْلَمُونَ نَسْتَعِينُ
Vocabulary
  • مِثْلُ ذَاlike this (the previous ruling)
  • عَرَضَappears, presents itself; something temporary
  • وَقْفًاdue to waqf (stop)
Translation
And like this, (madd will be jaiz) when the sukun is temporary due to waqf, like (in the examples) "ta'lamun" and "nasta'in."
Commentary

Madd 'arid li-l-sukun (the incidental madd due to a stop) is the second jaiz madd: a letter of madd is followed by a letter that only becomes sakin because the reciter has paused there, as in stopping on تَعْلَمُونَ or نَسْتَعِينُ. During continuous recitation (wasl), the final letter carries its normal vowel; the sukun, and therefore the madd, only "arises" at a pause. Its ruling is the same as madd munfasil: jaiz, allowing 2, 4, or 6 harakat.

Text
أَوْ قُدِّمَ الْهَمْزُ عَلَى الْمَدِّ وَذَا بَدَلْ كَآمَنُوا وَإِيمَانًا خُذَا
Vocabulary
  • قُدِّمَbefore
  • بَدَلْsubstitute, replace
  • خُذَاtake (them as examples)
Translation
Or (madd will be jaiz if) the hamzah is before the (letter of) madd. And this (madd) is badal, like "amanu" and "imanan," so take (them) as examples.
Commentary

Madd badal (the substitute madd) is the third jaiz madd, and it reverses the order seen in muttasil and munfasil: here the hamzah comes before the letter of madd, as in آمَنُوا or إِيمَانًا. These words were originally two consecutive hamzahs; the second, sakin hamzah is substituted with a letter of madd, which is why it is called "badal" (substitute). Qasr (2 harakat) is made according to all reciters including Hafs; a fuller pull is only found in the narration of Warsh.

The Three Jaiz Madds: munfasil (madd letter and hamzah in separate words), 'arid li-l-sukun (madd letter before a temporary pause-sukun), and badal (hamzah before the madd letter). All three permit either pulling or shortening, unlike muttasil, which is always compulsory.
