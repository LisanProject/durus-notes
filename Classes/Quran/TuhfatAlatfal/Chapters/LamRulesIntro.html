Chapter 4: The Lam of Al and the Lam of the Verb

This chapter covers two different lams that a reciter runs into constantly: the lam al-ta'rif, the definite article that turns an indefinite noun like "a pen" into "the pen," and the lam sakinah that appears inside verbs, whether past, present, or command. Both are silent letters, and both have their own rule for whether they are pronounced clearly or merged into what follows. Al-Jamzuri is careful to separate the two, since a beginner who has just learned that the definite article's lam merges before certain letters might wrongly assume the same applies inside a verb, which it does not.

Here is a list of the sections covered in this chapter.

Lam Qamariyyah: The fourteen "lunar" letters after which the lam of al is always pronounced clearly, gathered in a single memorable phrase.

Lam Shamsiyyah: The other fourteen "solar" letters after which the lam of al is assimilated entirely, merging into the letter that follows.

The Lam of the Verb: Why the lam sakinah inside a verb is always pronounced clearly, and the specific comparison to lam al-ta'rif that al-Jamzuri warns beginners against making.
