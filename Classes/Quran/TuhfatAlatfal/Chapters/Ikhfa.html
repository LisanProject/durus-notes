Ikhfa' الإخفاء

Text
وَالرَّابِعُ الْإِخْفَاءُ عِنْدَ الْفَاضِلِ *** مِنَ الْحُرُوْفِ وَاجِبٌ لِلْفَاضِلِ
فِيْ خَمْسَةٍ مِنْ بَعْدِ عَشْرٍ رَمْزُهَا *** فِيْ كِلْمِ هَذَا الْبَيْتِ قَدْ ضُمِّنْتُهَا
صِفْ ذَا ثَنَا كَمْ جَادَ شَخْصٌ قَدْ سَمَا …
Vocabulary
  • الْإِخْفَاءconcealment; partially hiding the noon sakinah or tanween, distinct from both idhhar and idgham
  • الْفَاضِل مِنَ الْحُرُوْفthe remaining letters; whatever is left over after the halq, idgham, and iqlab letters are accounted for
  • خَمْسَةٍ مِنْ بَعْدِ عَشْرٍfive after ten, i.e. fifteen; the count of letters that trigger ikhfa'
  • رَمْزُهَاtheir code, their cipher; referring to the mnemonic phrase that encodes all fifteen letters
Translation
The fourth (ruling) is ikhfa', for the remaining letters, obligatory for the remainder: in fifteen, whose code is contained in the words of this line: "Sif dha thana kam jada shakhsun qad sama..." (the mnemonic continues, encoding all fifteen letters).
Commentary

Ikhfa' applies by elimination: once the six halq letters (idhhar), the six yarmalun letters (idgham), and ba (iqlab) are set aside, fifteen letters remain, and every one of them triggers ikhfa'. Sheikh Hussein's correction on this point is worth internalizing directly: linguistically, "ikhfa'" means concealment (satr), not disappearance. The ghunnah remains present throughout, simply masked, the way the sun is masked by clouds without ceasing to exist. This is what distinguishes ikhfa' from "khafa'," complete absence.

Ikhfa' is the partial concealment of a noon sakinah or tanween: the sound is produced through the nose (ghunnah) with the tongue held away from noon's own articulation point, but also away from the following letter's point. It is a true middle state between the full clarity of idhhar and the full merging of idgham.

(5)
The Fifteen Ikhfa' Letters
Letters
ت ث ج د ذ ز س ش ص ض ط ظ ف ق ك

Three Degrees of Ikhfa': the strength of the nasalization varies with how close the following letter's articulation point is to noon's own. Followed by ت، د، ط (closest to noon's point), the ikhfa' is strongest. Followed by ق، ك (farthest from noon's point), it is weakest. The remaining ten letters produce an intermediate degree of ikhfa'.

(6)

Here are worked examples spanning the three degrees of ikhfa'.

Example 1 (Strongest ikhfa', with ta)Identify the noon sakinah or tanween, the letter that follows, and which degree of ikhfa' applies.مِن تَحْتِهَا

"from beneath it"

The noon sakinah in مِنْ is followed by ta in تَحْتِهَا. Ta is one of the three closest letters to noon's articulation point, so this produces the strongest degree of ikhfa', with the fullest nasalization.

Example 2 (Intermediate ikhfa', with sad)Identify the noon sakinah or tanween, the letter that follows, and which degree of ikhfa' applies.يَنصُرُكُمُ

"He supports you" (as in "if Allah supports you...")

The noon sakinah in يَنْ is followed by sad in صُرُكُمُ. Sad is an emphatic (mufakhkham) letter among the intermediate group, so the ikhfa' here is fuller and heavier than it would be before a light letter like seen, even though both fall in the same intermediate degree.

Example 3 (Weakest ikhfa', with qaf)Identify the noon sakinah or tanween, the letter that follows, and which degree of ikhfa' applies.مِن قَبْلُ

"from before"

The noon sakinah in مِنْ is followed by qaf in قَبْلُ. Qaf is one of the two farthest letters from noon's point, so this produces the weakest degree of ikhfa', with the lightest nasalization of the three degrees.

Example 4 (Tanween, strongest ikhfa')Identify the tanween, the letter that follows, and which degree of ikhfa' applies.قَوْمًا ظَلَمُوا

"a people who wronged"

The tanween at the end of قَوْمًا is followed by za in ظَلَمُوا. Za is one of the three closest letters to noon's point, so this is the strongest degree of ikhfa', applying to tanween exactly as it does to a written noon sakinah.

Example phrases above are illustrative of the three degrees of ikhfa' rather than citations of specific ayat; always verify wording and application against the mushaf and a qualified reciter. The mnemonic line for the fifteen ikhfa' letters is given here only as far as directly verified; confirm the remainder of the line with your own teacher's manuscript.
