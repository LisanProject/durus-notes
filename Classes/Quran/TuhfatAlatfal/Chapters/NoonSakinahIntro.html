Chapter 2: Rules of the Noon Sakinah and Tanween

This chapter covers the first major rulings of the poem: what happens to a noon sakinah (a noon carrying no vowel) or tanween (the double-vowel marking indefiniteness) when it meets the letter that follows it. Al-Jamzuri gives exactly four possible outcomes, each triggered by a specific, memorized set of letters. Everything else in Tuhfat al-Atfal assumes you can sort any given noon sakinah or tanween into one of these four categories on sight, so this chapter is worth mastering fully rather than skimming.

Here is a list of the sections covered in this chapter.

Idhhar Halqi (Clear Pronunciation): The six throat letters that require the noon sakinah or tanween to be pronounced clearly, with no nasalization or merging.

Idgham (Assimilation): The six letters of "yarmalun" that cause the noon sakinah or tanween to merge into the letter that follows, split into idgham with ghunnah and idgham without ghunnah, plus the four exceptions in a single word.

Iqlab (Conversion): What happens when a noon sakinah or tanween is followed by the letter ba, converting the sound to a nasalized meem.

Ikhfa' (Concealment): The remaining fifteen letters, which trigger a partial concealment of the noon sakinah or tanween, neither fully clear nor fully merged.
