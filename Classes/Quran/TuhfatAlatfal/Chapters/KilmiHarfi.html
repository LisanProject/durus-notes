Kilmi and Harfi, Mukhaffaf and Muthaqqal الكلمي والحرفي، المخفف والمثقل

Text
أَقْسَامُ لَازِمٍ لَدَيْهِمْ أَرْبَعَهْ وَتِلْكَ كِلْمِيٌّ وَحَرْفِيٌّ مَعَهْ
كِلَاهُمَا مُثَقَّلٌ مُخَفَّفُ فَهَذِهِ أَرْبَعَةٌ تُفَصَّلُ
Vocabulary
  • أَقْسَامُ لَازِمٍthe types of lazim
  • لَدَيْهِمْaccording to them, i.e. the reciters
  • كِلْمِيٌّkilmi; occurring within a word
  • حَرْفِيٌّharfi; occurring in the huruf muqatta'at
  • مُثَقَّلٌmuthaqqal; heavy
  • مُخَفَّفُmukhaffaf; light
  • تُفَصَّلُwill be explained/detailed
Translation
The types of lazim according to them (the reciters) are four; and it is kilmi and harfi with it. Both of them are muthaqqal and mukhaffaf, so these four will be explained.
Commentary

Madd lazim divides first into kilmi (word-based) and harfi (letter-based, referring to the huruf muqatta'at), and each of those divides again into muthaqqal (heavy) and mukhaffaf (light), producing four total combinations.

TypeTrigger
Kilmi MuthaqqalMadd letter followed by an original sukun caused by a doubled (mushaddad) letter, within a word
Kilmi MukhaffafMadd letter followed by an original sukun that is not doubled, within a word (rare)
Harfi MuthaqqalThe middle letter of a huruf muqatta'at spelling is a madd letter, and the third letter is doubled
Harfi MukhaffafThe middle letter of a huruf muqatta'at spelling is a madd letter, and the sukun is not doubled
Text
فَإِنْ بِكِلْمَةٍ سُكُونٌ اجْتَمَعْ مَعْ حَرْفِ مَدٍّ فَهُوَ كِلْمِيٌّ وَقَعْ
Vocabulary
  • فَإِنْ بِكِلْمَةٍso when, in a word
  • اجْتَمَعْjoins, meets, comes together
  • حَرْفِ مَدٍّa letter of madd
  • وَقَعْoccurred, has taken place
Translation
So when, in a (single) word, a sukun meets a letter of madd, then it is kilmi that has occurred.
Commentary

Kilmi occurs when a letter of madd is followed by a permanent sukun within a single ordinary word, most often produced by a shaddah, as in الضَّالِّينَ or الطَّامَّةُ.

Text
أَوْ فِي ثُلَاثِيِّ الْحُرُوفِ وُجِدَا وَالْمَدُّ وَسْطَهُ فَحَرْفِيٌّ بَدَا
Vocabulary
  • ثُلَاثِيِّ الْحُرُوفِtripled letters; huruf muqatta'at spelled out with three letters
  • وُجِدَاthey (the letter of madd and the sukun) are found
  • وَسْطَهُits center, the middle letter
  • بَدَاclear, apparent
Translation
Or in the tripled letters they (the letter of madd and the sukun) are found, while the (letter of) madd is in its center, then harfi is clear.
Commentary

Harfi occurs specifically within the huruf muqatta'at, the isolated letters opening certain surahs. Of these, only the ones spelled out with three letters where the middle letter is itself a letter of madd qualify, e.g. the noon in نون or the sad in صاد.

Text
مَا لَمْ يُدْغَمْ إِذَا لَكِلٍّ إِنْ أُدْغِمَا مَا لَمْ يُدْغَمَا
Vocabulary
  • إِنْ أُدْغِمَاif idgham takes place
  • لِكُلٍّeach one of them, both kilmi and harfi
  • مَا لَمْ يُدْغَمَاif no idgham occurs
Translation
Both (kilmi and harfi) are muthaqqal if idgham takes place. Mukhaffaf, each one of them, if no idgham occurs.
Commentary

With kilmi and harfi defined, this line adds the second axis: whichever of the two applies, if idgham occurs (the letter of madd's sukun merging into a following identical letter, producing a shaddah), the madd is muthaqqal (heavy), since a shaddah is a "heavier" pronunciation than a plain sukun. If no idgham occurs, it is mukhaffaf (light) instead, since a plain sukun is lighter to read than a doubled letter.

Muthaqqal vs. Mukhaffaf: muthaqqal applies when idgham produces a shaddah after the letter of madd, e.g. وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ. Mukhaffaf applies when no such doubling occurs, e.g. آلْآنَ.

(1)

Verses, vocabulary, and translation above are checked against Saleem Gaibie's Sharh Tuhfat al-Atfal (Madbut Writers and Translators, 3rd printing, 2014); commentary is paraphrased rather than quoted.

Here are worked examples applying the kilmi/harfi and mukhaffaf/muthaqqal classification.

Example 1 (Kilmi Muthaqqal)Identify the letter of madd, the sukun, and classify this madd lazim.وَلَا الضَّالِّينَ

"nor of those who have gone astray"

The alif in الضَّالِّينَ is followed by a doubled (mushaddad) lam within a single ordinary word. Since this occurs in a word (kilmah) and the following sukun comes from idgham (the shaddah), this is kilmi muthaqqal.

Example 2 (Harfi Muthaqqal)Identify the letter that opens this surah and classify its madd.الم

Alif-Lam-Meem, the opening of Surat al-Baqarah and several other surahs

The lam in this combination, spelled out as "lam" (lam-alif-meem), has the alif of madd in its middle followed by a meem that is doubled when joined to what follows. This is harfi (occurring in the huruf muqatta'at) and muthaqqal (idgham present), one of the eight letters of "kam 'asal naqas."
