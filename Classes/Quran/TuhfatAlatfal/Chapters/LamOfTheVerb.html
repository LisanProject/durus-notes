The Lam of the Verb لام الفعل

Text
وَأَظْهِرَنَّهُ فِي فِعْلٍ مُطْلَقَا فِي نَحْوِ قُلْ نَعَمْ وَقُلْنَا وَالْتَقَى
Vocabulary
  • وَأَظْهِرَنَّهُbe sure to make it clear; referring to the lam
  • فِي فِعْلٍin a verb
  • مُطْلَقَاgenerally, absolutely; in every case
  • فِي نَحْوِin for example
Translation
Be sure to make clear the lam of the verb in general, in for example: "qul na'am," "qulna," and "iltaqa."
Commentary

Having covered lam al-ta'rif in full, al-Jamzuri turns to the lam sakinah that appears inside verbs, whether past tense, present tense, or command, and states its rule plainly: it is always read with izhar, no matter what letter follows it. This holds even when the letter that follows is one that would trigger idgham if this were a lam al-ta'rif instead.

The Lam of the Verb is read with izhar unconditionally, regardless of the letter that follows it. This applies whether the lam falls in the middle of a verb or at its end, and in every tense: past, present, or command.

(3)

Al-Jamzuri states this rule explicitly because a beginner might otherwise reason by analogy from lam al-ta'rif: since the lam of "al" merges before noon in a word like "an-najm," a student might assume a verb's lam should merge before noon too. It does not. Likewise, "al-taqa" written with the definite article merges its lam before ta, but the verb "iltaqa" (he met) keeps its lam fully clear even though it also ends in a lam followed eventually by letters that would be shamsi in the other context. The lam of the verb simply does not follow the qamariyyah/shamsiyyah split at all; it is always izhar, full stop.

Here are worked examples of the lam of the verb.

Example 1Identify the lam sakinah and confirm it is read with izhar.قُلْ نَعَمْ

"Say: yes"

The lam sakinah in the command verb قُلْ is followed by noon in نَعَمْ. Even though noon is a shamsi letter for the definite article, this lam belongs to a verb, so izhar applies regardless: the lam is pronounced clearly.

Example 2Identify the lam sakinah and confirm it is read with izhar.وَالْتَقَى الْجَمْعَانِ

"and the two forces met"

The lam sakinah inside the past tense verb الْتَقَى is followed by qaf. This lam is internal to a verb form (iftaʿala), not the definite article, so it is read with izhar regardless of what follows, exactly as al-Jamzuri specifies.
