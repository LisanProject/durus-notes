Imam al-Sanusi

Notes on Muqaddimat al-Sanusi, taught by Sidi Shaykh Abdullahi Ahmed. I joined this class fairly late and will need to update the notes from the earlier sessions, but the bulk of what I've learned so far has covered the introductory definitions and classifications that ground the rest of the aqidah primer: the impediment, customary rulings, and rational rulings.

Topics

  1. Wajib, Mustahil, and Jaiz Explained - al-mani', the four types of customary connection, and the three categories of rational ruling: necessity, impossibility, and possibility.
  2. Jabariyyah, Qadariyyah, and Ahl al-Sunnah on Kasb - the three doctrines on human action, the meaning of kasb, and how free will fits with divine decree.
  3. The Six Types of Shirk Explained - defining ibadah and shirk, whether sujud is always shirk, intercession, the soul, and the six types of shirk with their rulings.
