The Three Rational Laws

For each scenario below, identify which of the three rational laws, Causality, Congruity, or Inseparability, is being applied, and explain why.

  1. A student argues that the universe's order and precision are themselves evidence that it was brought about deliberately, since order of that kind does not arise from nothing. Solution
  2. A critic objects: "Why assume the effect must resemble its cause in scale? A small cause could produce a large effect just as easily." Solution
  3. Explain why a chain of dominoes falling cannot be used as a complete analogy for how Allah brings about change in the universe. Solution
  4. A person claims that existence, power, will, and knowledge could each stand alone in a living being, independent of one another. Explain what rational law this denies. Solution
