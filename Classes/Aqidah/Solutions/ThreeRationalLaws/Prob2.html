2. A critic objects: "Why assume the effect must resemble its cause in scale? A small cause could produce a large effect just as easily."

The objection misunderstands the Law of Congruity (munasabah): an effect must match its cause. A universe requiring a cause capable of originating something of that magnitude requires a cause possessing power proportionate to it, unlimited power to originate something without any prior material or precedent. It is not a claim about literal physical size; it is a claim that the cause must actually be sufficient to explain the effect, not merely coincidentally connected to it.
