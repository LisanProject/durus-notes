4. A person claims that existence, power, will, and knowledge could each stand alone in a living being, independent of one another. Explain what rational law this denies.

This denies the Law of Inseparability (talazum): if one thing in a necessary chain of attributes is absent, the whole chain collapses. A being without ability, will, and knowledge at all cannot be alive, so life (hayah) itself necessarily brings the others with it, existence, power, will, knowledge, and life follow each other in that order, each one necessarily entailing the next rather than standing independently.
