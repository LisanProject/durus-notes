5. Someone says a masterfully composed poem could, in principle, be produced by someone with no skill in poetry at all, given enough luck. Which law does this violate, and why?

This violates the Law of Congruity (munasabah): an effect must match its cause. To produce a flawless poem, the effect (the poem) must match the cause, meaning it requires genuine skill in poetry as its source, since the effect (technical mastery on the page) cannot exceed or bypass what its cause is actually capable of. Attributing it to luck alone breaks the required correspondence between cause and effect.
