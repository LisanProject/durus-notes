The Prophets and the Unseen (Sam'iyyat)

Lesson commentary for this chapter has not been recorded yet — the matn and translation below are posted now so the primary text has a home on the site, with fuller commentary to follow once this material is actually covered in the lessons with Sidi Shaykh Muhammad al Maliki.

Text
ويجب للأنبياء عليهم الصلاة والسلام العصمة، فلا يقع منهم مخالفة لله في أمره ونهيه، وكذلك الملائكة. ويجب للرسل عليهم الصلاة والسلام تبليغ ما أمروا بتبليغه الخلائق من الأحكام وغيرها.
Translation
It is obligatory for the Prophets, upon them be peace and blessings, to possess 'isma, that is, protection from committing any violation of what Allah has commanded or forbidden. Likewise for the angels. It is obligatory for the Messengers, upon them be peace and blessings, to convey to creation everything they were commanded to convey of rulings and other matters.
Text
كاليوم الآخر وما فيه من: الحساب، والعقاب، والصراط، والميزان، والجنة، والنار، والعرش، والكرسي، وبالكتب السماوية والرسل وما وقع لهم من أممهم، وبالحور العين، والولدان، والأولياء، وبإسرائه صلى الله عليه وسلم، وبالمعراج، وبأن الشهداء أحياء عند ربهم يُرزقون، وبشفاعة نبينا محمد صلى الله عليه وسلم، وبعلامات الساعة، وتجديد التوبة من الذنوب، والرضا بالقضاء والقدر.
Translation
This includes belief in the Last Day and what it contains: the Reckoning, the Punishment, the Bridge, the Scale (al Mizan), the Garden, and the Fire. And in the Throne and the Footstool. And in the heavenly books and the messengers and what befell them from their communities. And in the houris and the eternal youths. And in the awliya'. And in the Night Journey of the Prophet, may Allah bless him and grant him peace, and in the Ascension. And that the martyrs are alive with their Lord and provided for. And in the intercession of our Prophet Muhammad, may Allah bless him and grant him peace. And in the signs of the Hour. And in the renewal of repentance from sins. And in acceptance of and contentment with divine decree.
A Brief Orientation

This closing section of the matn moves from what must be affirmed for Allah — the subject of every chapter before this one — to two further categories of required belief: what must be affirmed for the Prophets and Messengers, and what is traditionally called al sam'iyyat, the matters known only through revealed report rather than independent reason, chiefly concerning the unseen realities of the afterlife.

'Isma, protection from sin, is affirmed for prophets and angels alike, meaning neither category ever actually violates what Allah has commanded or forbidden. Messengers carry the further specific obligation of tabligh, conveying in full whatever revealed rulings and guidance they were charged with delivering to their communities, without concealment or alteration.

The list of sam'iyyat items that follows — the Reckoning, the Bridge, the Scale, the Garden and the Fire, the Throne and the Footstool, the houris, the martyrs being alive and provided for, the Prophet's intercession, the signs of the Hour, and the rest — are all matters affirmed on the authority of revelation rather than derived by the kind of rational argument used in earlier chapters for Allah's own attributes. That they are known through report rather than reason is not a mark of lesser certainty within this framework; as covered in Chapter 9 of these notes, matters transmitted with sufficient reliability (whether mutawatir or corroborated ahad) are treated as yielding the same certainty required of 'aqidah generally.
