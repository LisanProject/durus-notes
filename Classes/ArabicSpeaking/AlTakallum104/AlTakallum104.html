Telling Time Drill

Unit 5 is built entirely on four attachable pieces: وَالنِّصْفُ (half past), وَالرُّبْعُ (quarter past), وَالثُّلُثُ (twenty past), إِلَّا رُبْعًا (quarter to), and إِلَّا ثُلُثًا (twenty to). Say each line aloud, then cover the English and go straight from the clock face to the sentence.

ClockArabicMeaning
1:00السَّاعَةُ الوَاحِدَةُ تَمَامًاOne o'clock sharp
2:15السَّاعَةُ الثَّانِيَةُ وَالرُّبْعُQuarter past two
3:20السَّاعَةُ الثَّالِثَةُ وَالثُّلُثُTwenty past three
4:30السَّاعَةُ الرَّابِعَةُ وَالنِّصْفُHalf past four
5:40السَّاعَةُ السَّادِسَةُ إِلَّا ثُلُثًاTwenty to six
6:45السَّاعَةُ السَّابِعَةُ إِلَّا رُبْعًاQuarter to seven

Drill: pick five random times and say each one out loud before checking the pattern above. Then do it again using صَبَاحًا / مَسَاءً to say whether it's morning or evening.

Speaking Practice

Unit 5 — Times and Prices

كَمِ السَّاعَةُ الآنَ؟
What time is it now? Answer with a full sentence using the patterns above.

مَتَى تَذْهَبُ إِلَى المَطَارِ، صَبَاحًا أَمْ مَسَاءً؟
When do you go to the airport, morning or evening? Drill صَبَاحًا × مَسَاءً.

كَمْ دُولَارًا تُرِيدُ أَنْ تُغَيِّرَ، وَمَا سِعْرُ الجُنَيْهِ اليَوْمَ؟
How many dollars do you want to exchange, and what's today's rate for the pound?

Role-play: at the مَكْتَبُ الصِّرَافَةِ, exchange a specific amount of دُولَار for جُنَيْه مِصْرِيّ, ask the موظف to count it back to you, and confirm the change.

Unit 6 — Vacation

أَيْنَ سَتَقْضِي عُطْلَتَكَ هَذَا العَامَ؟
Where will you spend your vacation this year? Answer with يَقْضِي and a place from Unit 6.

هَلْ تُفَضِّلُ السَّفَرَ بِالطَّائِرَةِ أَمْ بِالقِطَارِ؟
Do you prefer traveling by plane or by train? Practice يُسَافِرُ in the answer.

مَاذَا تَلْتَقِطُ مِنْ صُوَرٍ عِنْدَمَا تُشَاهِدُ الأَهْرَامَ؟
What photos do you take when you watch the pyramids? Practice يَلْتَقِطُ and يُشَاهِدُ.

Role-play: you just got back from Alexandria. Describe three things you did using the past tense of يَسْتَمْتِعُ بِـ, يَزُورُ, and يَسْتَرِيحُ, while a partner asks follow-up questions.
