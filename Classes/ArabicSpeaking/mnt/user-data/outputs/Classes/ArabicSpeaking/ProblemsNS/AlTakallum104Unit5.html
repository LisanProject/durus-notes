104 Unit 5 — Times and Prices

Speaking and writing practice for Al-Takallum Elementary Unit 5. Say the speaking items out loud before checking the model answer; any true, grammatically sound answer that uses the target structure counts as correct, the model answer is one example, not the only one.

  1. Say the following time out loud in Arabic: 3:45. (اُنْطُقِ السَّاعَةَ التَّالِيَةَ)
  2. Speaking: answer in a full sentence. أَيْنَ تَذْهَبُ صَبَاحًا، وَأَيْنَ تَذْهَبُ مَسَاءً؟ (Where do you go in the morning, and where do you go in the evening?)
  3. Role-play (speaking, out loud): you are at the currency exchange office. Ask the employee to change 100 US dollars into Egyptian pounds, then ask for today's rate.
  4. Conjugate يَذْهَبُ for أَنْتِ in both the present and the past tense, then use the past-tense form in one full sentence about yesterday.
  5. Writing: write three sentences describing your daily schedule, each using a different time expression from Unit 5 (e.g. وَالنِّصْفُ, وَالرُّبْعُ, إِلَّا رُبْعًا).
  6. Writing: write a short four-line dialogue between a customer and a bank employee exchanging Kuwaiti dinars for Egyptian pounds, including a question about the change.
