Chapter 1: Al-Muqaddima

Al-Waraqat opens with definitions rather than rulings, and it is worth taking them seriously rather than rushing past them. Every later chapter assumes you already know precisely what asl, far', fiqh, hukm, and the various grades of knowledge and evidence mean. This chapter covers the foundational vocabulary of the whole text: the terms asl and far' from the title itself, the seven categories a ruling can fall into, the vocabulary of knowledge and ignorance that underlies how fiqh actually reasons, and the definition of usul al-fiqh together with a preview of every chapter still to come.

Here is a list of the sections covered in this chapter.

Foundational Terms – Al-asl, al-far', and the precise definition of fiqh as knowledge of shar'i rulings reached through ijtihad.

The Seven Ahkam – Wajib, mandub, mubah, mahzur, makruh, sahih, and fasid, with the reward or punishment attached to each.

Knowledge and Ignorance – Ilm, jahl, necessary versus acquired knowledge, nazar, istidlal, dalil, zann, and shakk, the vocabulary that describes exactly how certain, or uncertain, a piece of legal reasoning is.

Usul al-Fiqh and Its Chapters – The definition of usul al-fiqh itself, and the full list of topics the rest of the text, and this course, will cover.
