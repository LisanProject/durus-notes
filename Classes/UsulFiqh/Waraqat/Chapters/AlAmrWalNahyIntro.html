Chapter 3: Al-Amr wa'l-Nahy

This chapter covers command and prohibition, the two categories of speech that most directly generate legal rulings. Only the first section, the definition of command itself, is posted so far.

Here is a list of the sections covered so far in this chapter.

Al-Amr (Command) – The definition of a command, the default reading of the bare imperative as obligation, and the rules on repetition, immediacy, and the means an obligation depends on.

Al-nahy (prohibition), who is and is not addressed by command and prohibition, and the rest of this chapter will be added as they are covered in class.
