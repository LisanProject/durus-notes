Chapter 3: Al-Amr wa'l-Nahy

This chapter covers command and prohibition, the two categories of speech that most directly generate legal rulings.

Here is a list of the sections covered so far in this chapter.

Al-Amr (Command): The definition of a command, the default reading of the bare imperative as obligation, and the rules on repetition, immediacy, and the means an obligation depends on.

Who Is Addressed by Command, and Al-Nahy: Ahl al-taklif, who is and is not addressed by command and prohibition, why disbelievers are still addressed by the branches of the law, the reciprocal relationship between amr and nahy, and the definition of nahy itself.
