Al-Amr (Command)

Text
وَالْأَمْرُ: اسْتِدْعَاءُ الْفِعْلِ بِالْقَوْلِ مِمَّنْ هُوَ دُونَهُ عَلَى سَبِيلِ الْوُجُوبِ، وَالصِّيغَةُ الدَّالَّةُ عَلَيْهِ: افْعَلْ، وَهِيَ عِنْدَ الْإِطْلَاقِ وَالتَّجَرُّدِ عَنِ الْقَرِينَةِ تُحْمَلُ عَلَيْهِ، إِلَّا مَا دَلَّ الدَّلِيلُ عَلَى أَنَّ الْمُرَادَ مِنْهُ النَّدْبُ أَوِ الْإِبَاحَةُ فَيُحْمَلُ عَلَيْهِ. وَلَا يَقْتَضِي التَّكْرَارَ عَلَى الصَّحِيحِ إِلَّا إِذَا دَلَّ الدَّلِيلُ عَلَى قَصْدِ التَّكْرَارِ، وَلَا يَقْتَضِي الْفَوْرَ، وَالْأَمْرُ بِإِيجَادِ الْفِعْلِ أَمْرٌ بِمَا لَا يَتِمُّ الْفِعْلُ إِلَّا بِهِ، كَالْأَمْرِ بِالصَّلَوَاتِ أَمْرٌ بِالطَّهَارَةِ الْمُؤَدِّيَةِ إِلَيْهَا وَإِذَا فُعِلَ يَخْرُجُ الْمَأْمُورُ عَنِ الْعُهْدَةِ.
Vocabulary
  • اسْتِدْعَاءa calling for, a requesting
  • الصِّيغَةthe grammatical form; here, the imperative form افعل
  • الْقَرِينَةa contextual indicator that shifts a word away from its default meaning
  • النَّدْبrecommendation, a weaker implication than obligation
  • الْفَوْرimmediacy; acting right away rather than at any later point
  • الْعُهْدَةliability, the state of still owing an obligation
Translation
A command is the seeking of an action, by speech, from someone of lesser standing than the speaker, on the basis of obligation. The form that indicates it is "do," and when it appears unqualified and free of any contextual indicator, it is read as obligation, unless evidence shows the intended meaning is recommendation or mere permissibility, in which case it is read that way instead. On the correct view, it does not imply repetition unless evidence points to repetition being intended, and it does not imply immediacy. A command to bring about an action is also a command to do whatever that action cannot be completed without, the way a command to pray is also a command to perform the purification that leads to it, and once that is done, the one commanded is discharged of the obligation.
Commentary

Several real default rules are packed into this one passage, each of which shows up constantly in actual legal reasoning.

1. Al-Amr (Command) is the seeking of an action, by speech, from someone of lesser standing than the speaker, on the basis of obligation. The bare imperative form, with nothing else going on around it, defaults to obligation. That default only shifts if there is an actual qarina, a contextual signal, pointing elsewhere, toward recommendation or plain permissibility.

(1)

2. No Repetition, No Immediacy: a command does not, by itself, require the action to be repeated, and it does not require the action to happen immediately. Both of those only change if there is specific evidence pointing that way.

(2)

3. Muqaddima al-Wajib is the principle that whatever a command's fulfillment depends on is itself commanded. Since valid prayer depends on wudu, the command to pray is also, implicitly, a command to perform wudu, even though wudu is never named directly in the verse commanding prayer.

(3)
