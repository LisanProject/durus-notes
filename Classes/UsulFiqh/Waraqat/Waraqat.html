Al-Waraqat

الورقات في علم أصول الفقه · Imam al-Haramayn al-Juwayni

I am studying this text with Sidi Shaykh Abdullahi. Al-Waraqat is the standard first text in usul al-fiqh, short and dense, mostly definitions and rules with very few examples of its own. It is almost always studied alongside a commentary rather than alone. These notes lean on Sharh al-Waraqat by Jalal al-Din al-Mahalli, since that is the specific commentary edition these notes are drawn from, but two other commentaries are worth having alongside it as you go further: Qurrat al-'Ayn bi Sharh Waraqat al-Haramayn, and al-Mahalli's commentary in its fuller printed editions. Checking a point against more than one commentary is usually more useful than reading the same one twice.

One thing worth noting here. Before starting this book with Sidi Shaykh Abdullahi, I took a class on it with a local instructor in my area, and found it genuinely strange that he taught directly from al-Mahalli's own commentary while repeatedly rebuking al-Mahalli for his Ash'ari positions and tendencies. What I was actually looking for was an ijazah in the content itself, not merely an ijazah in transmission, which only requires reciting a text back correctly and says nothing about whether you understood or agreed with any of it. Sitting through a class where the author is corrected and disagreed with line after line felt like an inefficient and wasteful use of time toward that goal, so I left those classes and continued the text elsewhere.

Chapters

Here is a list of the chapters covered in this book.

Chapter 1: Al-Muqaddima: Foundational terms, the seven ahkam, the vocabulary of knowledge and ignorance, and the definition of usul al-fiqh with its full list of chapters.

Chapter 2: Aqsam al-Kalam: The structure of Arabic speech, and the distinction between literal and figurative usage.

Chapter 3: Al-Amr wa'l-Nahy: Command and prohibition. Only the definition of command is posted so far.

More chapters will be added here as they are covered in class.
