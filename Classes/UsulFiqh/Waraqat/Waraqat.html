Al-Waraqat

Al-Waraqat was written by Imam al-Haramayn al-Juwayni and is the standard first text in Usul al-Fiqh, the discipline that explains how rulings are actually derived from the Quran and Sunnah. It is short, mostly definitions and rules with very few examples, which is why it is usually read alongside a commentary. The lessons below follow the order of the text.

  1. Fiqh, Usul al-Fiqh, and Hukm - the basic terms the rest of the text depends on.
  2. The Seven Ahkam - wajib, mandub, mubah, haram, makruh, sahih, and batil.
  3. The Four Sources - Quran, Sunnah, Ijma', and Qiyas.
  4. Amr and Nahy - command and prohibition, and what each implies about a ruling.
  5. Amm and Khass - general and specific wording.
  6. Mujmal and Mubayyan - ambiguous wording and the wording that clarifies it.
  7. Zahir and Mu'awwal - apparent meaning versus interpreted meaning.
  8. Af'al al-Nabi - the actions of the Prophet, peace be upon him, as a source of law.
  9. Naskh - abrogation, and how one ruling can replace another.
  10. Al-Ijma' - consensus, and its standing as a source of law.
  11. Al-Akhbar - mutawatir and ahad reports.
  12. Al-Qiyas - analogy, and the four pillars it depends on.
  13. Ijtihad, Taqlid, and Fatwa - who is qualified to derive rulings, and who is not.
