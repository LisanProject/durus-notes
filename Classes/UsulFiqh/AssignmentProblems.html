Usul al-Fiqh - Assignment Problems

Assignment problems for Usul al-Fiqh. These mirror the Practice Problems in topic and difficulty but without solutions shown, meant for self-testing or assigning to someone else. Full worked solutions for the same topics are on the Practice Problems page.

Al-Waraqat

Chapter 1: Al-Muqaddima

  1. Define al-asl and al-far' in your own words, and give an example of each different from any used in the notes.
  2. A ruling is agreed upon by every school without exception. Is it "fiqh" in the technical sense defined in this chapter? Explain why or why not.
  3. List all seven ahkam and, for each, state whether doing the act, leaving the act, or both carries a reward or punishment.
  4. Explain the difference between nufudh and i'tidad, and give an example of an act that is sahih in each sense.
  5. What is the difference between necessary knowledge and acquired knowledge? Which category does fiqh fall into, and why?
  6. Distinguish zann from shakk. Which one describes the epistemic state of ijtihad, and what hadith supports this?
  7. List the fifteen chapters of usul al-fiqh named in this chapter, in order.

Chapter 2: Aqsam al-Kalam

  1. Name the four types of majaz, and identify which type applies to وَاسْأَلِ الْقَرْيَةَ (Yusuf 12:82).
  2. What is the difference between haqiqa lughawiyya and haqiqa shar'iyya? Give an example of each.

Chapter 3: Al-Amr wa'l-Nahy

  1. State the default legal weight of a bare command with no contextual indicator, and explain what would shift that default toward recommendation instead.
  2. Does a command by itself require immediate action? What is this principle called, and what would override the default?

Full solutions to these same topics, phrased somewhat differently, are on the Practice Problems page.
