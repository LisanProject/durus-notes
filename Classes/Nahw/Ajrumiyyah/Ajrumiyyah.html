Al-Ajrumiyyah

المقدمة الآجرومية في علم النحو · The Ajurrumian Introduction to the Science of Grammar

Imam Muhammad ibn Muhammad ibn Dawud al-Sanhaji, known as Ibn Ajurrum, 672-723 AH / 1273-1323 CE

Translated and annotated by L.A. Hussein, with notes drawn from the lessons of Sidi Shaykh Abdullahi, may Allah preserve him and extend his benefit. This is a working document prepared by a student of knowledge. It is not a scholarly edition and is not a substitute for study under a qualified teacher. Errors will be present, and corrections are welcomed.

Translator's Preface

This project grew from a recognition that the foundational sciences of Arabic syntax (nahw) and morphology (sarf) are not preliminary obstacles to be cleared before reaching the real sciences. They are themselves among the most consequential things a student of the tradition can acquire. Every science of the din depends on them. Tafsir, fiqh, usul, hadith, and aqida are all, at their core, exercises in reading Arabic correctly. A student who cannot parse a sentence precisely cannot evaluate a legal argument, distinguish between possible readings of a verse, or understand why scholars disagreed about a given question. The tools of nahw and sarf are not supplementary and are absolutely crucial, as Sidi Shaykh Abdullahi has mentioned throughout my time with him.

His lessons form the basis of these notes, and I have found that he teaches grammar in the way it was always meant to be taught. His focus is to treat the science as a discipline that opens texts, not as an end in itself. Every definition learned and completed is preparation for sitting with a text of Quran, hadith, or fiqh and understanding what it actually says, not what one assumes it says. The goal of these notes is to make that preparation accessible to students who may not yet have found their way to a teacher, with the hope that the notes serve as a companion to direct study rather than a substitute for it.

This is a working document. Errors will be present and corrections are welcomed. The reader is urged in all matters to return to a qualified teacher and to treat these notes as what they are: a companion to study, not a substitute for it.

وَمَا تَوْفِيقِى إِلَّا بِٱللَّهِ ۚ عَلَيْهِ تَوَكَّلْتُ وَإِلَيْهِ أُنِيبُ

And my success comes only through Allah. In Him I trust and to Him I turn.

Chapters

Here is a list of the chapters covered in this book.

Chapter 1: Al-Kalam – What counts as kalam, the three word categories, and the concrete signs used to identify each one.

Chapter 2: Al-I'rab – The definition of i'rab and the eight word categories with their own dedicated case signs, then the signs of rafa and the signs of nasb in detail.

More chapters will be added here as they are translated and annotated.
