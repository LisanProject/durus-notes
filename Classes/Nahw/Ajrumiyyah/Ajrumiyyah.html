Liban's Online Notes

Ibn Ajurrum al-Sanhaji

The entry point of the classical Nahw curriculum. Notes include heavy I'rab practice on short sentences, building the habit of grammatical analysis from the very beginning.

Topics

  1. Introduction & the Word (الكلمة) - Definition of speech (الكلام), its divisions: noun, verb, particle. Signs of each. (sample lesson posted)
  2. I'rab - Case Endings (الإعراب) - Marfu', mansub, majrur, majzum. Visible and estimated endings.
  3. The Mubtada' & Khabar (المبتدأ والخبر) - Nominal sentences. Subject and predicate, their types and rules.
  4. Nawasikh - inna & kana (النواسخ) - Verbs and particles that alter the nominal sentence.
  5. The Fa'il & Na'ib al-Fa'il (الفاعل ونائبه) - The doer of the action; passive voice and its agent.
  6. Mansubat - Accusative Categories - Maf'ul bihi, maf'ul mutlaq, zarf, hal, tamyiz, mustathna, and more.
  7. Al-Majrurat - Genitive Categories - Prepositions (huruf al-jarr) and idafah (possessive construction).
