Al-Ajrumiyyah

The Ajrumiyyah is the standard first text in Nahw, written by ibn Ajurrum. It lays out kalam, the three word types, the signs used to identify each one, and a first pass at i'rab. The lessons below follow the order I am reviewing it in.

  1. Kalam - what counts as speech in the grammatical sense, and the four conditions a word must meet.
  2. Word Types (Ism, Fi'l, Harf) - the three categories every Arabic word falls into.
  3. Signs of the Ism - the markers that tell you a word is an ism.
  4. Signs of the Fi'l - the markers that tell you a word is a fi'l, and which type.
  5. Al-I'rab: Definition and Types - what i'rab is, and the four states: raf', nasb, khafd, jazm.
  6. Signs of I'rab - how each of the four states is actually marked on a word.
  7. Al-Mu'rabat - inflected words, and the distinction between mu'rab bi-l-harakat and mu'rab bi-l-huruf.
  8. Bab al-Af'al - the chapter on verbs and how they take i'rab.
  9. Al-Marfu'at I - al-fa'il and na'ib al-fa'il.
  10. Al-Marfu'at II - al-mubtada wa-l-khabar, and kana and inna with their sisters.
  11. Al-Marfu'at III - the tawabi': na't, 'atf, tawkid, and badal.
  12. Al-Mansubat - the accusative cases, maf'ul bihi through khabar kana.
  13. Al-Majrurat - the genitive cases, majrur bi-l-harf and majrur bi-l-idafah.
