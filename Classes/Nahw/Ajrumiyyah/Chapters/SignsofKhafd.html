Signs of Khafd علامات الخفض

Text
وَلِلْخَفْضِ ثَلَاثُ عَلَامَاتٍ: الْكَسْرَةُ، وَالْيَاءُ، وَالْفَتْحَةُ. فَأَمَّا الْكَسْرَةُ، فَتَكُونُ عَلَامَةً لِلْخَفْضِ فِي ثَلَاثَةِ مَوَاضِعَ: فِي الِاسْمِ الْمُفْرَدِ الْمُنْصَرِفِ، وَجَمْعِ التَّكْسِيرِ الْمُنْصَرِفِ، وَفِي جَمْعِ الْمُؤَنَّثِ السَّالِمِ. وَأَمَّا الْيَاءُ، فَتَكُونُ عَلَامَةً لِلْخَفْضِ فِي ثَلَاثَةِ مَوَاضِعَ: فِي الْأَسْمَاءِ الْخَمْسَةِ، وَفِي التَّثْنِيَةِ، وَالْجَمْعِ. وَأَمَّا الْفَتْحَةُ، فَتَكُونُ عَلَامَةً لِلْخَفْضِ فِي الِاسْمِ الَّذِي لَا يَنْصَرِفُ.
Vocabulary
  • الْخَفْضkhafd, the genitive case, used for nouns after a preposition or in an idafa construction
  • مُنْصَرِفmunsarif, a noun that accepts tanwin and kasra
  • غَيْر مُنْصَرِفghayr munsarif, a diptote noun that does not accept tanwin or kasra
  • جَمْع التَّكْسِيرjam' al-taksir, a broken plural formed by an internal change to the singular's pattern
  • جَمْع الْمُؤَنَّث السَّالِمjam' al-mu'annath al-salim, the sound feminine plural, formed by adding ات
Translation
Khafd has three signs: the kasra, the ya, and the fatha. The kasra is a sign of khafd in three places: the singular declining noun, the declining broken plural, and the sound feminine plural. The ya is a sign of khafd in three places: the five nouns, the dual, and the plural. The fatha is a sign of khafd in the noun that does not decline.
Commentary

Khafd is the case used for a noun governed by a preposition or standing as the second term of an idafa construction. Like nasb, it has more than one possible sign, but unlike nasb, one of its signs, the fatha, only appears because a whole category of noun is barred from taking kasra at all.

1. Al-Kasra is the root sign of khafd, appearing on the singular declining noun (al-ism al-mufrad al-munsarif), the declining broken plural (jam' al-taksir al-munsarif), and the sound feminine plural (jam' al-mu'annath al-salim). Munsarif here means a noun that is able to accept tanwin and kasra in the first place, a status not every noun has.

(1)

2. Al-Ya is the sign of khafd for the five nouns (al-asma al-khamsa), the dual (al-tathniyah), and the sound masculine plural (jam' al-mudhakkar al-salim). These are exactly the same three categories that take alif for nasb and waw for rafa, so the ya here is simply the khafd form of the same huruf-based declension pattern.

(2)

3. Al-Fatha is the sign of khafd for the noun that does not decline (al-ism alladhi la yansarif), meaning a ghayr munsarif noun. A ghayr munsarif noun cannot take tanwin or a kasra at all, so in khafd position it substitutes a fatha instead of the kasra a regular noun would take. Foreign proper names are the most common example, since a name like Ibrahim does not accept tanwin the way an ordinary Arabic noun like rajul does.

(3)
