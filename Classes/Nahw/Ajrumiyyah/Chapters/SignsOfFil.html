Signs of the Fi'l علامات الفعل

Text
وَالْفِعْلُ يُعْرَفُ بِقَدْ، وَالسِّينِ، وَسَوْفَ، وَتَاءِ التَّأْنِيثِ السَّاكِنَةِ. وَالْحَرْفُ مَا لَا يَصْلُحُ مَعَهُ دَلِيلُ الِاسْمِ وَلَا دَلِيلُ الْفِعْلِ
Vocabulary
  • قَدْthe particle qad; attaches only to verbs; with the past tense it means "indeed" or confirms the action occurred; with the present tense it expresses expectation or possibility
  • السِّينthe sin; a single letter prefix indicating the near future, e.g. سَيَكْتُبُ (he will write, soon)
  • سَوْفَsawfa; a separate word indicating the more distant future, e.g. سَوْفَ يَكْتُبُ (he will write, eventually)
  • تَاء التَّأْنِيث السَّاكِنَةthe silent feminine ta; the sukun-bearing ta that attaches to the end of a past tense verb to mark a feminine subject, e.g. كَتَبَتْ (she wrote); called silent because it carries a sukun, not a vowel
  • دَلِيلsign; identifier; the marker that confirms a word belongs to a given category
  • يَصْلُحis compatible; fits; is appropriate for
Translation
The verb is identified by qad, the sin, sawfa, and the silent feminine ta. The particle is that with which neither the sign of the noun nor the sign of the verb is compatible.
Commentary

The verb is identified by accepting any of four markers. None of these can attach to a noun or a particle, so their presence on a word confirms it is a verb.

1. Qad (قَدْ) is a particle that attaches only to verbs. With a past tense verb it conveys confirmation, قَدْ كَتَبَ means "he has indeed written." With a present tense verb it conveys expectation or possibility, قَدْ يَكْتُبُ means he may write. Because qad can only attach to verbs it serves as a clear identifier.

(1)

2. Al-Sin and Sawfa (س / سَوْفَ) are both future markers and attach only to present tense verbs. The sin indicates the near future and sawfa the more distant future. Both are exclusive to verbs and so confirm a word's verbal identity when present.

(2)

3. Ta al-Ta'nith al-Sakinah (The Silent Feminine Ta) attaches to the end of past tense verbs to indicate a feminine subject. It is called silent because it carries a sukun rather than a vowel, كَتَبَتْ (she wrote). This ta is distinct from the ta of the second person masculine, كَتَبْتَ (you wrote) which carries a fatha and is not a sign of the verb in this sense.

(3)

The particle has no positive signs of its own. It is identified purely by exclusion: if a word accepts neither the signs of the noun nor the signs of the verb, it is a particle. Ibn Ajurrum does not list signs for the particle because there are none to list. The particle is what remains after the noun and verb have been accounted for.

The commentary shows qad carrying two different meanings depending on which tense it attaches to.

Example 6Identify the sign that shows this word is a verb.قَدْ أَفْلَحَ الْمُؤْمِنُونَ

"The believers have certainly succeeded" (al-Mu'minun 23:1).

Step 1: Identify the particle attached to أَفْلَحَ: it is قَدْ, which attaches only to verbs.
Step 2: Determine the tense of the verb it enters upon: أَفْلَحَ is past tense.
Step 3: Recall what qad conveys with a past tense verb: confirmation that the action has definitely occurred.

Conclusion: أَفْلَحَ is a verb, identified by قَدْ entering upon it, and here qad conveys certainty rather than possibility because the verb is in the past tense.

Example 7Identify the sign that shows this word is a verb.قَدْ يَصْدُقُ الْكَذُوبُ

"The liar sometimes tells the truth."

Step 1: Identify the particle attached to يَصْدُقُ: again قَدْ.
Step 2: This time check the tense: يَصْدُقُ is present tense, not past.
Step 3: Recall what qad conveys with a present tense verb: expectation or possibility, i.e. that the action happens only sometimes.

Conclusion: يَصْدُقُ is a verb, identified by qad, and here qad's meaning flips from confirmation (as in Example 6) to rarity, purely because the tense of the verb changed.

Example 8Identify the sign that shows this word is a verb.سَيَقُولُ السُّفَهَاءُ مِنَ النَّاسِ

"The foolish among the people will say" (al-Baqarah 2:142).

Step 1: Identify the marker attached to the front of سَيَقُولُ: the single-letter prefix سَ.
Step 2: Confirm this is one of the two future markers listed in the text (the sin and sawfa), and that it attaches only to a present tense verb.
Step 3: The sin specifically marks the near future, distinguishing it from sawfa.

Conclusion: سَيَقُولُ is a verb, identified by the sin prefix, which marks the near future.

Example 9Identify the sign that shows this word is a verb.وَلَسَوْفَ يُعْطِيكَ رَبُّكَ فَتَرْضَىٰ

"And your Lord is going to give you, and you will be satisfied" (al-Duha 93:5).

Step 1: Identify the marker preceding يُعْطِيكَ: the separate word سَوْفَ, not the single-letter sin.
Step 2: Confirm it attaches to a present tense verb, just like the sin does.
Step 3: Recall the distinction between the two: sawfa marks the more distant future, in contrast to the sin's near future.

Conclusion: يُعْطِيكَ is a verb, identified by سَوْفَ, which here marks a more distant future than the sin would.

Example 10Identify the sign that shows this word is a verb.قَالَتْ عَائِشَةُ أُمُّ الْمُؤْمِنِينَ

"Aisha, Mother of the Believers, said."

Step 1: Identify the marker attached to the end of قَالَتْ: the silent ta, تْ, carrying a sukun.
Step 2: Confirm it attaches only to a past tense verb, and only to mark a feminine subject.
Step 3: Distinguish it from the second-person ta (as in كَتَبْتَ), which carries a fatha and marks person rather than gender; that ta is not one of the four signs of the verb.

Conclusion: قَالَتْ is a verb, identified by the silent feminine ta, which here marks that the subject, Aisha, is feminine.
