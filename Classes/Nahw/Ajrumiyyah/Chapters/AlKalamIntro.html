Chapter 1: Al-Kalam

This chapter covers the opening section of the matn: what counts as kalam in the technical sense, the three categories every Arabic word falls into, and the concrete signs used to tell which category a given word belongs to. Everything later in the book, i'rab included, assumes you can already sort a word into ism, fi'l, or harf on sight, so this chapter is worth genuinely mastering rather than skimming.

Here is a list of the sections covered in this chapter.

Kalam – The Basmala and the formal definition of kalam, the four conditions a word must meet to count as grammatically complete speech.

Word Types (Ism, Fi'l, Harf) – The three categories every Arabic word falls into, with the basic definition and examples of each.

Signs of the Ism – The four markers, khafd, tanwin, the definite article, and the prepositions, that identify a word as a noun, with real examples for each including several drawn directly from the Quran.

Signs of the Fi'l – The four markers, qad, the sin, sawfa, and the silent feminine ta, that identify a word as a verb, and how the particle is defined purely by process of elimination.
