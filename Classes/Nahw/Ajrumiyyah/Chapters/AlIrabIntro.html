Chapter 2: Al-I'rab

This chapter introduces i'rab itself, the change that occurs at the end of a word depending on what governs it, and then works through the signs of all four of its states: rafa, nasb, khafd, and jazm. Before getting into the signs, the chapter first covers eight word categories that behave differently from the ordinary singular noun, since almost every sign discussed afterward is stated in terms of these categories. It closes by sorting every declinable word into the two broader groups they actually belong to: those declined by vowel change, and those declined by letter change.

Here is a list of the sections covered in this chapter.

I'rab, Definition and the Word Categories covers the eight word categories with their own dedicated case signs, followed by the formal definition of i'rab and its four states, illustrated with the same name carried through raf', nasb, and khafd in three sentences.

Signs of Rafa covers the four signs of the nominative case, the damma, the waw, the alif, and the nun, with the places each one applies.

Signs of Nasb covers the five signs of the accusative case, the fatha, the alif, the kasra, the ya, and dropping the nun, with real Quranic examples for several of them.

Signs of Khafd covers the three signs of the genitive case, the kasra, the ya, and the fatha, including the munsarif/ghayr munsarif distinction that decides which sign a given noun takes.

Signs of Jazm covers the two signs of the jussive mood, the sukun and the hadhf, which apply only to the present tense verb.

Al-Mu'rabat: Declined by Vowels or by Letters ties the whole chapter together by sorting every declinable word into the vowel-declined group or the letter-declined group, with the three exceptions to the vowel pattern.
