Word Types أقسام الكلمة

Text
وَأَقْسَامُهُ ثَلَاثَةٌ: اسْمٌ، وَفِعْلٌ، وَحَرْفٌ جَاءَ لِمَعْنًى
Vocabulary
  • أَقْسَامdivisions. Plural of قِسْم
  • اسْمnoun. A word with meaning in itself, not bound to a time
  • فِعْلverb. A word with meaning in itself, bound to a time
  • حَرْفparticle. A word with meaning only in relation to others
  • جَاءَ لِمَعْنًىthat carries a meaning. Distinguishing meaningful particles from mere letters
Translation
Its divisions are three. They are noun, verb, and particle that carries a meaning.
Commentary

Every word in Arabic is one of three things. It is a noun, a verb, or a particle. This division was arrived at by methodical study (istiqra') of the entire Arabic corpus and has no valid exception in classical usage.

1. Ism (Noun) is a word with meaning in itself, not bound to a time. For example محمد, كتاب.

(1)

2. Fi'l (Verb) is a word with meaning in itself, bound to a time. For example كَتَبَ (he wrote), يَكْتُبُ (he writes).

(2)

3. Harf (Particle) is a word with meaning only in relation to others. For example فِي (in), مِنْ (from).

(3)
