Al-Mu'rabat: Declined by Vowels or by Letters فَصْلُ الْمُعْرَبَاتِ

Text
اَلْمُعْرَبَاتُ قِسْمَانِ: قِسْمٌ يُعْرَبُ بِالْحَرَكَاتِ، وَقِسْمٌ يُعْرَبُ بِالْحُرُوفِ. فَاَلَّذِي يُعْرَبُ بِالْحَرَكَاتِ أَرْبَعَةُ أَنْوَاعٍ: اَلِاسْمُ الْمُفْرَدُ، وَجَمْعُ التَّكْسِيرِ، وَجَمْعُ الْمُؤَنَّثِ السَّالِمِ، وَالْفِعْلُ الْمُضَارِعُ الَّذِي لَمْ يَتَّصِلْ بِآخِرِهِ شَيْءٌ. وَكُلُّهَا تُرْفَعُ بِالضَّمَّةِ وَتُنْصَبُ بِالْفَتْحَةِ وَتُخْفَضُ بِالْكَسْرَةِ وَتُجْزَمُ بِالسُّكُونِ. وَخَرَجَ عَنْ ذَلِكَ ثَلَاثَةُ أَشْيَاءَ: جَمْعُ الْمُؤَنَّثِ السَّالِمِ يُنْصَبُ بِالْكَسْرَةِ، وَالِاسْمُ الَّذِي لَا يَنْصَرِفُ يُخْفَضُ بِالْفَتْحَةِ، وَالْفِعْلُ الْمُضَارِعُ الْمُعْتَلُّ الْآخِرِ يُجْزَمُ بِحَذْفِ آخِرِهِ. وَالَّذِي يُعْرَبُ بِالْحُرُوفِ أَرْبَعَةُ أَنْوَاعٍ: التَّثْنِيَةُ، وَجَمْعُ الْمُذَكَّرِ السَّالِمِ، وَالْأَسْمَاءُ الْخَمْسَةُ، وَالْأَفْعَالُ الْخَمْسَةُ، وَهِيَ: يَفْعَلَانِ، وَتَفْعَلَانِ، وَيَفْعَلُونَ، وَتَفْعَلُونَ، وَتَفْعَلِينَ.
Vocabulary
  • اَلْمُعْرَبَاتal-mu'rabat, words that take grammatical case inflection (i'rab) at all, as opposed to mabni words, which are fixed
  • حَرَكَاتharakat, the short vowels: damma, fatha, kasra, and their absence, sukun
  • حُرُوفhuruf, letters, here used as the actual sign of a case rather than a vowel
  • خَرَجَ عَنْ ذَلِكَ"departed from that", the standard phrase introducing exceptions to a just-stated rule
Translation
The declinable words are two kinds: a kind declined by vowels, and a kind declined by letters. The one declined by vowels is four types: the singular noun, the broken plural, the sound feminine plural, and the present tense verb with nothing attached to its ending. All of these take rafa with damma, nasb with fatha, khafd with kasra, and jazm with sukun. Three things depart from that: the sound feminine plural takes nasb with kasra, the non-declining noun takes khafd with fatha, and the present tense verb with a weak ending takes jazm by dropping its ending. The one declined by letters is four types: the dual, the sound masculine plural, the five nouns, and the five verbs, which are: yaf'alani, taf'alani, yaf'alun, taf'alun, and taf'alin.
Commentary

This section ties together everything covered across Signs of Rafa, Signs of Nasb, Signs of Khafd, and Signs of Jazm, by sorting every declinable word into exactly two groups: those whose case shows up as a vowel change, and those whose case shows up as a letter change.

1. Declined by Vowels (Bi'l-Harakat). Four categories default to this pattern: the singular noun, the broken plural, the sound feminine plural, and the present tense verb with nothing attached to its ending. The default rule for all four is damma for rafa, fatha for nasb, kasra for khafd, and sukun for jazm.

(1)

2. The Three Exceptions. Even within the vowel-declined group, three specific cases break from the default pattern rather than following it directly. The sound feminine plural takes nasb with kasra rather than fatha. The non-declining (ghayr munsarif) noun takes khafd with fatha rather than kasra, exactly as covered under Signs of Khafd. The present tense verb with a weak ending takes jazm by dropping its final letter (hadhf) rather than by sukun, exactly as covered under Signs of Jazm.

(2)

3. Declined by Letters (Bi'l-Huruf). Four categories are declined this way instead of by vowels: the dual (rafa with alif, nasb and khafd with ya), the sound masculine plural (rafa with waw, nasb and khafd with ya), the five nouns (rafa with waw, nasb with alif, khafd with ya), and the five verbs (rafa by the retained nun, nasb and jazm by dropping it). Every one of these four categories was already introduced individually across the previous Signs sections; this is the first place they are grouped together as a single structural class.

(3)
