Al-Ajrumiyyah

Lesson I: Introduction to Grammar

Identify each word below as ism, fi'l, or harf, and give the reason.

1. بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ

بِ is a harf, a preposition attaching to the noun that follows it. اسْمِ is an ism, identified by the khafd it takes after the preposition. اللَّهِ is an ism, a proper noun majrur by annexation to اسْمِ. الرَّحْمَنِ and الرَّحِيمِ are both ism, identified by the definite article ال, and stand as descriptions (na't) of اللَّهِ, majrur to agree with it.

2. قَدْ يَكْتُبُ زَيْدٌ

قَدْ is a harf, since it attaches only to verbs. يَكْتُبُ is a fi'l, identified precisely by qad entering upon it. زَيْدٌ is an ism, identified by tanwin.

Lesson III: Signs of I'rab

Identify the sign of i'rab on the underlined word and name the category it belongs to.

3. قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى

اللَّهُ is marfu' as the fa'il of قَالَ. The sign is damma, since it is a singular noun.

4. الْمُسْلِمُونَ صَادِقُونَ

الْمُسْلِمُونَ is marfu' as mubtada. The sign is waw, since it is a sound masculine plural.

5. رَأَيْتُ الْمُسْلِمِينَ

الْمُسْلِمِينَ is mansub as maf'ul bihi. The sign is ya, since it is a sound masculine plural, which takes ya rather than fatha in nasb.

6. رَأَيْتُ الْمُسْلِمَاتِ

الْمُسْلِمَاتِ is mansub as maf'ul bihi. The sign is kasra, since it is a sound feminine plural, which takes kasra rather than fatha in nasb.

7. Surat al-Hajj 22:2 contains وَتَرَى النَّاسَ سُكَارَىٰ. Identify the case of سُكَارَىٰ and explain why its sign is not visible.

سُكَارَىٰ is mansub as the second object of تَرَى. It is a broken plural, and its sign of nasb is an implicit fatha, since a word ending in alif maqsura cannot physically carry a visible fatha.

8. Surat al-Nur 24:32 contains وَأَنكِحُوا الْأَيَامَىٰ مِنكُمْ. What category does الْأَيَامَىٰ belong to, and why is its fatha implicit rather than visible?

الْأَيَامَىٰ is a broken plural, mansub as the object of أَنكِحُوا. As with سُكَارَىٰ above, the fatha is implicit because the word ends in alif maqsura, which cannot take a visible case vowel.

9. Surat Taha 20:91 contains لَن نَّبْرَحَ عَلَيْهِ عَاكِفِينَ. Identify what makes نَّبْرَحَ mansub and name its sign.

نَّبْرَحَ is a present tense verb made mansub by the particle لَن, which governs the accusative in a following verb. Since nothing is attached to its end, its sign is a visible fatha.

The remaining questions below are translated directly from al-Tuhfat al-Saniyyah's own review questions for this section (translated here from the Arabic, not from any published English edition).

10. In how many places is the fatha a sign of nasb?

Three: the singular noun, the broken plural, and the present tense verb preceded by a nasib with nothing attached to its end.

11. Give four examples of a mansub singular noun: one masculine with explicit fatha, one masculine with implicit fatha, one feminine with explicit fatha, and one feminine with implicit fatha.

Masculine, explicit: لَقِيتُ عَلِيًّا. Masculine, implicit: لَقِيتُ الْفَتَى. Feminine, explicit: قَابَلْتُ هِنْدًا. Feminine, implicit: وَدَّعْتُ لَيْلَى.

12. Give four different examples of a mansub broken plural.

صَاحَبْتُ الرِّجَالَ، رَعَيْتُ الْهُنُودَ (both explicit fatha), وَتَرَى النَّاسَ سُكَارَىٰ، وَأَنكِحُوا الْأَيَامَىٰ (both implicit fatha, since both end in alif maqsura).

13. When is the mudari' verb made mansub by fatha?

When it is preceded by a nasib particle, such as لَنْ or أَنْ, and nothing else is attached to its end.

14. Give two different examples of a mansub mudari' verb.

لَن نَّبْرَحَ عَلَيْهِ عَاكِفِينَ (explicit fatha) and يُرْضِينِي أَنْ تَسْعَى إِلَى الْمَجْدِ (implicit fatha).

15. What sign of nasb does the mudari' verb take when the alif of duality is attached to its end?

Dropping the nun rather than fatha, e.g. لَنْ يَضْرِبَا.

16. If a mudari' verb preceded by a nasib has a confirming nun attached to its end, what is its ruling?

It becomes fixed (mabni) on fatha while remaining in the position of nasb, e.g. وَاللَّهِ لَنْ تَذْهَبَنَّ.
