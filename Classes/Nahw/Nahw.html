Arabic Sciences (Nahw and Sarf)

Here are my notes for Nahw and Sarf, the Arabic grammar and morphology that everything else in the Islamic sciences rests on. I am studying these under Sidi Shaykh Abdullahi, and I have tried to make the notes self contained, though they do assume you are working through the texts in order rather than jumping in at the middle.

A couple of notes on how these are organized. Each book below gets its own page listing its lessons in order. Within a lesson you will usually find the Arabic text, a translation, a short vocabulary list, and commentary, following the same structure I use in my own study notes. Some books further down the list do not have lessons posted yet. That is not an oversight, I just have not gotten to them.

Al-Ajrumiyyah

The Ajrumiyyah is the first text in the sequence, and covers the basics of kalam, the three word types, and an introduction to i'rab.

Go to the Al-Ajrumiyyah lesson list

Qatr al-Nada

Qatr al-Nada follows the Ajrumiyyah and goes into more depth on the same topics. Notes for this book are coming soon.
