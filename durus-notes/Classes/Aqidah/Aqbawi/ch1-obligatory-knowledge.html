Chapter 1 — Obligatory Knowledge upon the Mukallaf

Download this chapter (PDF) Download full book (PDF)

The Matn

اعْلَمْ أَنَّ مَعْرِفَةَ اللهِ وَاجِبَةٌ عَلَى كُلِّ مُكَلَّفٍ، وَذَلِكَ بِمَعْرِفَةِ صِفَاتِهِ الوَاجِبَةِ وَالمُسْتَحِيلَةِ وَالجَائِزَةِ، وَكَذَلِكَ مَعْرِفَةُ صِفَاتِ رُسُلِهِ.
Know that knowledge of Allah is obligatory upon every legally responsible person (mukallaf), and this is by knowing His attributes: those that are necessary (wajib), impossible (mustahil), and permissible (ja'iz). Likewise obligatory is knowledge of the attributes of His Messengers.

Commentary

Al-'Aqbawi opens with the foundational obligation of the entire science: every legally responsible Muslim must have ma'rifa — conscious, reasoned knowledge — of Allah. This is not mere imitation (taqlid) of one's parents or community.

The word mukallaf means any sane, adult Muslim who has reached the age of legal responsibility (bulugh). The obligation falls on this person — children and the insane are not held to it.

The three categories — wajib, mustahil, ja'iz — are the organising framework of the entire discipline. Every attribute of Allah and every attribute of the Prophets will be classified through this lens in every text we study.

The dominant Ash'ari position — which al-'Aqbawi is expressing — is that taqlid is not sufficient for the core articles. A person must have at least some reasoned basis for their belief, not merely follow their parents.

Scholars differ on how much reasoning is required. Some hold that a brief rational reflection suffices; others require familiarity with the standard proofs. What is agreed is that the obligation exists and cannot be fulfilled by taqlid alone.

Al-Bajuri's hashiya on the Jawharah and the commentary on Umm al-Barahin both address this question in detail.

Key Terms

Arabic Transliteration Meaning
المكلَّف al-Mukallaf The legally responsible person: sane, adult Muslim.
واجب Wajib Necessary — what must be the case. Its opposite is mustahil.
مستحيل Mustahil Impossible — what cannot be attributed to Allah or the Prophets.
جائز Ja'iz Permissible/possible — neither necessary nor impossible.
معرفة Ma'rifa Conscious, reasoned knowledge — as opposed to taqlid.
