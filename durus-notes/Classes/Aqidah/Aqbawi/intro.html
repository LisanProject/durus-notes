Sharh al-'Aqbawi — Introduction & Author

Download full book (PDF)

The Text

Sharh al-'Aqbawi is a prose commentary on the creed of Abu 'Abdillah Muhammad ibn Yusuf al-Sanusi (the same scholar who wrote Umm al-Barahin). Al-'Aqbawi wrote his commentary as an accessible introduction to Ash'ari theology, making it the natural starting point for students of 'aqidah before moving on to the verse texts.

The Author

Shams al-Din Muhammad al-'Aqbawi (d. 880 AH / 1480 CE) was a North African Maliki scholar and theologian. His commentary is known for its clarity and accessibility — he explains the core Ash'ari positions without assuming prior knowledge of kalam terminology.

How to Use These Notes

  • Each chapter page begins with the Arabic matn and an English translation.
  • Commentary follows, explaining the meaning and context.
  • Use Show / Hide buttons to expand detailed discussions when needed.
  • Key terms are listed in a table at the end of each chapter.
  • Cross-references point to the same topic in Jawharah, Kharidah, and Umm al-Barahin.
Prerequisite No prior knowledge of kalam is assumed. However, basic Arabic literacy helps for reading the matn. All passages are fully translated.
