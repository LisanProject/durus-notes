Learning From Your Errors

Mistakes in these sciences are not just something to feel bad about, they are information. A wrong i'rab, a misapplied fiqh ruling, a misremembered hadith chain, each one is telling you specifically where your understanding is thin.

  • Keep a running log of your mistakes. A short, ongoing list of errors you have actually made, not hypothetical ones, is one of the most efficient study tools available. It tells you exactly what to prioritize in review.
  • Figure out why, not just what. Getting a case ending wrong because you misidentified the grammatical position is a different problem than getting it wrong because you forgot the sign. The fix is different in each case.
  • Correct it immediately, in writing. Do not just nod along when a teacher corrects you. Write the correction down right away, ideally next to the original note, so the correction survives longer than the moment of correction.
  • Revisit your error log before it repeats. A mistake you have made once is worth rechecking before the next session where it is likely to come up again.
  • Do not let embarrassment stop you from asking again. Everyone studying these sciences gets things wrong regularly, including advanced students. Treating a mistake as shameful rather than useful mostly just slows down the correction.
