General Tips

  1. Attend consistently. Missing a session means you are catching up on top of whatever comes next. In a cumulative curriculum this compounds fast.
  2. Study on a schedule, not in bursts. A short review most days beats a long cram once a week. This is especially true for memorization, which depends on spaced repetition more than raw hours.
  3. Do not just read, engage. Read the Arabic out loud. Write out the i'rab of a sentence yourself before checking the answer. Translate a passage cold before looking at a translation.
  4. Keep your materials in one place. Scattered notes across notebooks, PDFs, and memory are hard to review. Pick a system, Scrivener, a binder, whatever works, and stick to it.
  5. Ask questions when something does not make sense. A grammatical rule or a fiqh ruling that seems inconsistent almost always has a reason behind it. Write the question down if you cannot ask right away, and actually follow up on it.
  6. Do not skip ahead in the curriculum. The order of classical texts is deliberate. A later text usually assumes fluency with everything in the text before it.
