Memorization

Memorization is central to how these sciences have always been transmitted, and it is worth treating as its own skill rather than something that just happens as a side effect of studying.

  • Memorize in small, consistent chunks. A few lines a day, reviewed daily, beats memorizing a large passage in one sitting and letting it decay.
  • Recite out loud. Silent memorization is much less durable than memorization reinforced by hearing your own voice say the words correctly.
  • Layer your review. New material needs daily review. Once it is solid it can move to a weekly cycle, and eventually a monthly one. Trying to review everything you have ever memorized every single day does not scale.
  • Understand before you memorize where possible. Memorizing a line whose meaning you already grasp sticks better, and is far less likely to be recited with a mistake that changes the meaning without you noticing.
  • Test yourself, do not just re-read. Recite from memory and only check the text when you get stuck. Reading along while "reviewing" a memorized passage feels productive but does much less for actual retention.
  • Build a realistic timeline. A longer text memorized over many months in a sustainable pace beats an ambitious deadline abandoned halfway through.
