Assignment Problems

These are problem sets posted without worked solutions, meant for a teacher to assign, or for testing yourself without the temptation of peeking at an answer. Arabic Sciences (Nahw and Sarf)

Al-Ajrumiyyah

Lesson I: Introduction to Grammar

Identify each word below as ism, fi'l, or harf, and give the reason.

  1. بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَنِ الرَّحِيمِ
  2. قَدْ يَكْتُبُ زَيْدٌ

Lesson III: Signs of I'rab

Identify the sign of i'rab on the underlined word and name the category it belongs to.

  1. قَالَ اللَّهُ تَعَالَى
  2. الْمُسْلِمُونَ صَادِقُونَ
  3. رَأَيْتُ الْمُسْلِمِينَ
  4. رَأَيْتُ الْمُسْلِمَاتِ
  5. Surat al-Hajj 22:2 contains وَتَرَى النَّاسَ سُكَارَىٰ. Identify the case of سُكَارَىٰ and explain why its sign is not visible.
  6. Surat al-Nur 24:32 contains وَأَنكِحُوا الْأَيَامَىٰ مِنكُمْ. What category does الْأَيَامَىٰ belong to, and why is its fatha implicit rather than visible?
  7. Surat Taha 20:91 contains لَن نَّبْرَحَ عَلَيْهِ عَاكِفِينَ. Identify what makes نَّبْرَحَ mansub and name its sign.

The following are variations on al-Tuhfat al-Saniyyah's own review questions for this section, not the same questions.

  1. Name the three places where fatha is a sign of nasb, and give one original example of your own for each place.
  2. Give four new examples of a mansub singular noun, different from any used in the lesson: one masculine with explicit fatha, one masculine with implicit fatha, one feminine with explicit fatha, one feminine with implicit fatha.
  3. Find one additional Quranic example, beyond the two already cited in this lesson, of a broken plural made mansub with an implicit fatha.
  4. A mudari' verb is preceded by a nasib but is not mansub with a fatha. Explain what else must be true of the verb's ending for this to happen.
  5. Give two new examples, not from the lesson, of a mansub mudari' verb, one with an explicit fatha and one with an implicit fatha.
  6. What sign of nasb does the mudari' verb take when the waw of a masculine plural subject, rather than the alif of duality, is attached to its end?
  7. If a mudari' verb preceded by a nasib has the nun of the feminine plural attached to its end, rather than a confirming nun, what is its ruling?

Full solutions to problems 1 through 9 are on the Practice Problems page. Problems 10 through 16 above are new variations rather than the same questions with solutions removed, so they will not match the Practice Problems page one to one.
