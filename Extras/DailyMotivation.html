Daily Motivation from Irshad al-'Ibad

A short daily entry drawn from Irshad al-'Ibad ila Sabil al-Rashad ("Guiding Worshippers to the Path of Guidance"), the classical Shafi'i primer of Shaykh Zayn al-Din al-Malaybari, author of Fath al-Mu'in. Alongside its fiqh, the book is threaded with hadith, admonitions (mawa'iz), and short stories (hikayat) meant to keep the heart moving, not just the mind. Each entry here pairs the original Arabic with a translation and a short reflection, meant as a two minute reset before the rest of the day's study.

Never Slept in the Same Room as It

وحكى المالكي أن الإمام أبا بكر بن فورك ما نام في بيت فيه مصحف قط، وإذا أراد النوم انتقل عن المكان الذي فيه إعظاماً لكتاب الله عز وجل.
Al-Maliki narrated that Imam Abu Bakr ibn Furak never once slept in a house he was in if a mushaf was present in it. When he wanted to sleep he would move away from wherever it was, out of reverence for the Book of Allah, Mighty and Majestic.

Irshad al-'Ibad, Bab Fadl al-Qur'an.

Nobody is bound to this. It's just one scholar's own standard for himself, and it's worth asking what your own version of that standard would even look like.

Malik ibn Dinar's Tears

قال رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم: "ما من عبد يخطب خطبة إلا الله سائله عنها يوم القيامة ما أراد بها" قال، فكان مالك بن دينار إذا حدث بهذا بكى، ثم يقول: أتحسبون عيني تقر بكلامي عليكم، وأنا أعلم أن الله سائلي عنه يوم القيامة ما أردت به، فأقول: أنت الشهيد على قلبي لو لم أعلم أنه أحب إليك لم أقرأ على اثنين أبداً.
The Messenger of Allah, peace and blessings be upon him, said "there is no servant who gives a sermon except that Allah will question him on the Day of Judgment about what he meant by it." Whenever Malik ibn Dinar narrated this hadith he would weep, then say "do you think my eye finds any rest in speaking to you like this, while I know Allah will question me about it on the Day of Judgment, about what I actually meant by it. So I say to Him, You are witness over my heart. If I did not know it was something You loved, I would never have recited in front of two people again."

Irshad al-'Ibad, Bab al-'Ilm.

He taught constantly and still checked himself every single time he did it. That's the part worth sitting with before opening any book today.
