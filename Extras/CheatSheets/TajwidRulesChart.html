Tajwid Rules Chart
Noon Sakinah and Tanween
Idhhar Halqi الإظهار الحلقي
Pronounce noon/tanween clearly, no merging. Before: ء  ه  ع  ح  غ  خ
Idgham الإدغام
With ghunnah, before: ي  ن  م  و
Without ghunnah, before: ل  ر
Exception, one word only (idhhar mutlaq): دنيا، صنوان، قنوان، بنيان
Iqlab الإقلاب
Noon/tanween → hidden nasalized meem, before: ب
Ikhfa' الإخفاء
Partial concealment, ghunnah retained, before the remaining 15 letters:
ت ث ج د ذ ز س ش ص ض ط ظ ف ق ك
Strongest, before: ت د ط    Weakest, before: ق ك    Intermediate: remaining 10
Meem Sakinah
Ikhfa' Shafawi الإخفاء الشفوي
Light nasal concealment, before: ب
Idgham Shafawi الإدغام الشفوي
Merges into a following meem, before: م
Izhar Shafawi الإظهار الشفوي
Clear, before all other 26 letters (default case)
Caution: clear but easily blurred before waw and fa specifically

The Lam of Al and the Lam of the Verb
Lam Qamariyyah اللام القمرية
Clear (izhar). Mnemonic: ابغ حجك وخف عقيمه
ء ب غ ح ج ك و خ ف ع ق ي م ه
Lam Shamsiyyah اللام الشمسية
Assimilated, following letter doubled (shaddah)
ت ث د ذ ر ز س ش ص ض ط ظ ل ن
Lam of the Verb لام الفعل
Always izhar, no exceptions, regardless of what follows
e.g. قُلْ نَعَمْ, الْتَقَى
Letter Relationships
Mithlayn المتماثلان
Identical letters: same makhraj and sifat
Mutajanisayn المتجانسان
Same makhraj, differing sifat
Mutaqaribayn المتقاربان
Close makhraj, differing sifat
Saghir / Kabir الصغير / الكبير
Saghir: 1st letter sakin → idgham
Kabir: both vowelled → izhar (default)

Madd: Letters and Basic Types
Letters of Madd حروف المد
ا always after fatha
و sakin, after dammah
ي sakin, after kasrah
Madd Tabi'i (Asli) المد الطبيعي
No hamzah or sukun follows: 2 harakat, fixed
Madd al-Leen مد اللين
Waw/ya sakin, preceded by fatha (not madd's conditions)
e.g. خَوْفٌ, بَيْتٌ
Madd Far'i: The Rulings
Muttasil المتصل: Wajib
Hamzah after madd letter, same word: 4-5 harakat
Munfasil المنفصل: Jaiz
Hamzah after madd letter, next word: 2, 4, or 5 harakat
'Arid li-l-Sukun العارض للسكون: Jaiz
Sukun only at a pause (waqf): 2, 4, or 6 harakat
Badal البدل: Jaiz
Hamzah before the madd letter: usually 2 harakat
Lazim اللازم: Always Required
Permanent sukun (wasl and waqf both): always 6 harakat
Madd Lazim: Four Types
Kilmi: in an ordinary word    Harfi: in the huruf muqatta'at
Muthaqqal: idgham (shaddah) follows    Mukhaffaf: plain sukun

Huruf Muqatta'at (Surah Openers)
Madd Lazim Harfi, Mnemonic "كم عسل نقص"
ك م ع س ل ن ق ص : 6 harakat ('ayn also permits 4-5)
Madd Tabi'i Harfi, Mnemonic "حي طهر"
ح ي ط ه ر : 2 harakat only
No Madd
Alif: never elongated when spelled out alone
Common Recitation Slips
SlipCorrection
Applying ikhfa' shafawi before waw or faOnly meem triggers idgham shafawi, only ba triggers ikhfa' shafawi; waw/fa take izhar shafawi, just pronounced with care
Merging noon sakinah + yarmalun letter inside one wordIdhhar mutlaq applies instead in the four exception words: دنيا صنوان قنوان بنيان
Treating madd 'arid li-l-sukun as fixed at one durationIt is jaiz: 2, 4, or 6 harakat are all valid depending on tradition
Assuming lam inside any word follows qamariyyah/shamsiyyahThat split only applies to lam al-ta'rif; the lam of a verb is always izhar
