Home

Welcome to my free online notes documenting my journey through the traditional Islamic sciences. The intent of this site is to provide structured, accessible notes and eventually downloadable references for anyone walking a similar path, whether studying formally under a teacher, attending circles at your local mosque, or learning independently.

The model for this site is Paul's Online Notes. I first found it as a college student working through Calculus and kept returning to it for years afterward. What made it useful was not just the content but the approach: clear explanations, well-organized topics, and a site that felt like it was written by someone who actually wanted you to understand. This is my attempt at that for the Islamic sciences.

The teachers who shaped me — in particular Sidi Shaykh Abdullahi Ahmed, Sidi Shaykh Muhammad al-Maliki, Sidi Shaykh Rabie Rashwan, Sidi Shaykh Hussein al-Masri, and Sidi Shaykh Ibrahim Niyass Shuru — embodied the Sunnah in the way they walked into a room, in the way they treated people, and in the light with which they spoke about the Prophet ﷺ. This site is, in one sense, my attempt to stay connected to what they passed on.

Most of the loudest voices speaking against traditional Sunni Islam today have never seriously engaged with the actual texts of those traditions. The Ashʿarī and Māturīdī schools together represent the carefully reasoned theological consensus of the vast majority of this ummah across eleven centuries. I hope this site plays some small role in changing that.

At present the notes cover Arabic Syntax and Morphology, Fiqh, Usūl al-Fiqh, Ashʿarī ʿAqīdah, Hadīth, and ʿUlūm al-Qurʾān. I would like to thank in advance anyone who writes in with corrections — they are a form of charity, and I am genuinely grateful for them.

All courses include notes and, once sufficiently complete, downloadable PDFs. Arabic sections include Iʿrāb practice sets. Fiqh and Usūl sections include worked examples where relevant.

Arabic Grammar

النحو والصرف Nahw & Sarf

Al-Ājurrūmiyyah — Ibn Ājurrūm al-Sanhājī [Notes] [Practice Problems] - Topics included in this set of notes are :

  • The Word & Its Divisions (الكلمة) - Definitions of ism, fiʿl, and ḥarf; signs distinguishing each type.
  • Iʿrāb — Case Endings (الإعراب) - Marfūʿ, manṣūb, majrūr, majzūm; visible vs. estimated endings; signs of each case.
  • The Mubtadaʾ & Khabar - Nominal sentence structure; the subject and predicate; conditions and types.
  • Nawāsikh — inna & kāna and their sisters - Particles and verbs that alter the nominal sentence; their governance.
  • The Fāʿil & Nāʾib al-Fāʿil - The doer of the verbal action; passive voice and its substitute agent.
  • Manṣūbāt — Accusative Positions - Mafʿūl bihi, mafʿūl muṭlaq, ẓarf al-zamān wal-makān, ḥāl, tamyīz, mustaṯnā, and more.
  • Al-Majrūrāt — Genitive Positions - Prepositions (ḥurūf al-jarr) and their letters; iḍāfah (possessive construction).

The entry point of the classical Nahw curriculum. Notes include heavy Iʿrāb practice on short sentences, building the habit of grammatical analysis from the very beginning. The Ājurrūmiyyah is the first text every student of Arabic grammar studies; its brevity is intentional — it establishes the skeleton that everything else builds on.

Qatr al-Nadā wa Ball al-Sadā — Ibn Hishām al-Ansārī [Notes] [Practice Problems] - Topics included in this set of notes are :

  • Definitions & Expanded Divisions of the Word - A more detailed treatment of ism, fiʿl, and ḥarf with their sub-types and signs.
  • Iʿrāb in Detail - Nine cases for strong nouns; irregular declension patterns; dual and plural iʿrāb.
  • The Complete Marfūʿāt - All nominative positions in Arabic syntax; their conditions and exceptions.
  • The Complete Manṣūbāt - All accusative positions; extended treatment of ḥāl, tamyīz, and mustaṯnā.
  • Particles & Their Grammatical Effects - Ḥurūf al-naṣb, ḥurūf al-jazm, ḥurūf al-jarr — their types and governance rules.
  • Qurʾānic Practice Exercises - Practice drawn directly from Qurʾānic sentences throughout.

A significant step up from the Ājurrūmiyyah. Practice problems draw directly from Qurʾānic sentences to reinforce that grammar is a tool for engaging with the Book of Allah. Ibn Hishām's treatment is fuller and more nuanced than the Ājurrūmiyyah while remaining accessible.

Qawāʿid al-Iʿrāb — Ibn Hishām al-Ansārī [Notes] [Practice Problems] - Topics included in this set of notes are :

  • Principles of Parsing (Iʿrāb) - The methodology of grammatical analysis; how to identify a word's position and state its evidence.
  • Particles & Their Parsing - How to parse every major particle; maḥall iʿrāb for particle-governed clauses.
  • Clauses (Jumal) & Their Grammatical Positions - When clauses have a maḥall iʿrāb and when they do not; the seven positions of the jumlah.
  • Qurʾānic Iʿrāb Exercises - Worked parsing of selected āyāt; applied exercises for each rule.

A focused text on the rules governing grammatical parsing. Iʿrāb practice is drawn entirely from the Qurʾān. This text is often studied alongside the Qatr and is indispensable for developing fluency in parsing.

Alfiyyat Ibn Mālik — Ibn Mālik al-Andalusī [Notes] [Practice Problems] - Topics included in this set of notes are :

  • Lines 1–50 — Kalām & Iʿrāb Foundations - Definitions of speech and the word; the three divisions; the cases of iʿrāb and their signs.
  • Lines 51–150 — Marfūʿāt - Mubtadaʾ, khabar, fāʿil, nāʾib al-fāʿil, and related topics in full detail.
  • Lines 151–300 — Nawāsikh - Kāna and her sisters; inna and her sisters; ẓanna and her sisters; laysa; mā al-Ḥijāziyyah.
  • Lines 301–500 — Manṣūbāt - All accusative categories; mafāʿīl, ḥāl, tamyīz, mustaṯnā, munādā, and exceptions.
  • Lines 501–700 — Majrūrāt & Tawābiʿ - Prepositional phrases, iḍāfah, naʿt, ʿaṭf, badal, tawkīd — the followers.
  • Lines 701–900 — Sarf (Morphology) - Verb patterns, maṣdar forms, verbal nouns, augmented forms, and their rules.
  • Lines 901–1000 — Iʿlāl & Abnormal Forms - Weak letters, substitution, elision, imālah, and the completion of the poem.

The central text of the Arabic grammar tradition. One thousand lines of rajaz verse encoding the complete rules of Nahw and Sarf. Practice works through the poem itself — parsing its verses so that memorization and grammatical understanding reinforce each other. Assumes completion of the Qatr al-Nadā.

Mughnī al-Labīb ʿan Kutub al-Aʿārīb — Ibn Hishām al-Ansārī [Notes] - Topics included in this set of notes are :

  • Part I — The Particles (الباب الأول) - Every Arabic particle, its meanings, and grammatical governance — arranged alphabetically with examples.
  • Part II — Compound Expressions (الباب الثاني) - Compound conjunctions, conditional structures, and their grammatical implications.
  • Parts III–VIII — Advanced Rules of Syntax - Eight chapters on higher syntactic rules, disputes among grammarians, and resolution of complex cases.

The summit of classical Nahw study covered on this site. An encyclopedic treatment of the particles and grammatical structures of Arabic. Notes at this level assume full completion of the Alfiyyah. This is a reference text as much as it is a study text.

Ashʿarī ʿAqīdah

العقيدة الأشعرية Ashʿarī Theology

Sharḥ al-ʿAqbāwī (ʿAqīdah Tawḥīdiyyah) — Al-ʿAqbāwī [Notes] - Topics included in this set of notes are :

  • The Attributes of Allah — Wājib, Mustaḥīl, Jāʾiz - The twenty necessary attributes and their opposites; what is merely possible for Allah.
  • The Attributes of the Messengers - Ṣidq, amānah, tablīgh, faṭānah and their opposites; what is impossible for a Prophet.

Notes in preparation.

Al-Kharīdah al-Bahiyyah — Al-Dardīr Memorize [Notes] - Topics included in this set of notes are :

  • Lines 1–20 — Necessary Attributes of Allah - Wujūd, qidam, baqāʾ, mukhālafah lil-ḥawādith, qiyām bi-l-nafs, waḥdāniyyah.
  • Lines 21–45 — Attributes of Meaning & Meaningful States - Qudrah, irādah, ʿilm, ḥayāh, samʿ, baṣar, kalām and the maʿānī / maʿnawiyyah distinction.
  • Lines 46–66 — The Prophets & Conclusion - Attributes of the prophets; what is obligatory for every mukallaf to know.

Notes in preparation. Target: full memorization of the 66-line poem.

Umm al-Barāhīn (Al-ʿAqīdah al-Sanūsiyyah) — Al-Sanūsī Memorize [Notes] - Topics included in this set of notes are :

  • Introduction — The Obligation of Knowledge & Rational Categories - The first obligation on the mukallaf; wājib, mustaḥīl, and jāʾiz as rational categories.
  • Wājib, Mustaḥīl, Jāʾiz for Allah - The twenty necessary, twenty impossible, and one possible attribute with their rational proofs.
  • Wājib, Mustaḥīl, Jāʾiz for the Prophets - The four necessary, four impossible, and one possible attribute of every messenger.
  • The Rational Proofs (Dalāʾil ʿAqliyyah) - Proof for each necessary attribute and refutation of its impossible opposite.

Notes in preparation. One of the most widely memorized creedal texts in the Ashʿarī tradition.

Al-Jawharat al-Tawḥīd — Al-Laqānī Memorize [Notes] - Topics included in this set of notes are :

  • Necessary Attributes — Extended Treatment - More detailed argumentation than in the Sanūsiyyah; distinctions between the attributes.
  • Prophethood (Nubuwwāt) - The prophets, their number, conditions of prophethood, and the question of miracles (muʿjizāt).
  • Eschatology (Samʿiyyāt) - What is known only through revelation: the Day of Judgment, the scales, the bridge, paradise, and hellfire.

Notes in preparation. A poem of approximately 144 lines — the summit of the Ashʿarī poetic theology tradition.

Hadīth

الحديث الشريف Texts & Memorization

Al-Arbaʿīn al-Nawawiyyah — Imām al-Nawawī Memorize [Notes] - Topics included in this set of notes are :

  • Ḥadīth 1–5 — Intentions & Foundations - Beginning with "Actions are by intentions." The foundational aḥādīth of the collection.
  • Ḥadīth 6–15 — Ḥalāl, Ḥarām & Doubt - The ḥadīth of Nuʿmān ibn Bashīr; legal and ethical principles drawn from each narration.
  • Ḥadīth 16–28 — Rights & Conduct - Treatment of anger, rights of the Muslim, avoiding harm, and related principles.
  • Ḥadīth 29–42 — Faith, Zuhd & Conclusion - The final aḥādīth covering tawbah, zuhd, and the perfection of character.

Target: full memorization. Notes include commentary, chain information, and related rulings derived from each ḥadīth.

Al-Shamāʾil al-Muḥammadiyyah — Al-Tirmidhī Memorize [Notes] - Topics included in this set of notes are :

  • Chapters 1–5 — Physical Description (Khalq) - The seal of prophethood, the Prophet's ﷺ hair, complexion, gait, and clothing.
  • Chapters 6–15 — Food, Drink & Household - What the Prophet ﷺ ate, drank, and how he lived at home.
  • Chapters 16–30 — Worship & Prayer - His prayer, his night prayer (tahajjud), his fasting, and his remembrance of Allah.
  • Chapters 31–56 — Character & Final Illness - His laughter, his speech, his humility, and the narrations of his passing ﷺ.

Target: full memorization. The most important book ever written about the person of the Prophet ﷺ — his appearance, his habits, his character. Reading this text transforms the student's relationship with the Prophet ﷺ.

Al-Muwaṭṭaʾ — Imām Mālik ibn Anas Memorize [Notes] - Topics included in this set of notes are :

  • Kitāb al-Ṭahārah - Purity; wuḍūʾ; what breaks it. The aḥādīth reflecting the practice of Madīnah.
  • Kitāb al-Ṣalāh - Times, call to prayer, imāmah, Jumu'ah, and the funeral prayer.
  • Kitāb al-Zakāh & al-Ṣawm - Zakāh thresholds; Ramaḍān; zakāt al-fiṭr.
  • Kitāb al-Ḥajj - Pilgrimage rituals according to the practice of the people of Madīnah.
  • Kitāb al-Buyūʿ onward - Transactions, marriage, divorce, inheritance, and the remaining chapters.

Target: full memorization. The oldest organized collection of hadith and fiqh, compiled by the Imām of the People of Madīnah. Al-Shāfiʿī said there is no book on earth after the Book of Allah more correct than the Muwaṭṭaʾ.

ʿUlūm al-Qurʾān

علوم القرآن Tajwīd & Qirāʾāt

Tuḥfat al-Aṭfāl — Sulaymān al-Jamzūrī Memorize [Notes] [Practice Problems] - Topics included in this set of notes are :

  • Lines 1–10 — Introduction & Nūn Sākinah / Tanwīn - The four rules: iẓhār, idghām (with and without ghunnah), iqlab, ikhfāʾ.
  • Lines 11–20 — Mīm Sākinah & Shaddah - Ikhfāʾ shafawī, idghām shafawī, iẓhār shafawī; ghunnah on the shaddah.
  • Lines 21–40 — Madd (Prolongation) - Types of madd: ṭabīʿī, muttaṣil, munfaṣil, ʿāriḍ lil-sukūn, and their lengths.
  • Lines 41–61 — Lām, Rāʾ & Conclusion - Rules of the lām and rāʾ; tafkhīm and tarqīq (heaviness and lightness).

A 61-line poem covering the foundational rules of Tajwīd. The standard entry point into formal Tajwīd study worldwide. Target: full memorization before moving to the Jazariyyah. These sciences cannot be learned from notes alone — a licensed teacher with an unbroken chain is a condition, not a recommendation.

Matn al-Jazariyyah — Ibn al-Jazarī (d. 833 AH) Memorize [Notes] - Topics included in this set of notes are :

  • Lines 1–20 — Makhārij al-Ḥurūf - The points of articulation for every letter of the Arabic alphabet.
  • Lines 21–50 — Ṣifāt al-Ḥurūf - The characteristics of letters: jahr/hams, shiddah/rikhwah, iṭbāq/infitāḥ, idhiāq/iṣmāt, and others.
  • Lines 51–80 — Rules of Nūn, Mīm & Madd - A more technically precise treatment than the Tuḥfah, with additional distinctions.
  • Lines 81–107 — Waqf, Ibtidāʾ & Conclusion - Rules of stopping and starting recitation; the famous closing lines on the obligation of tajwīd.

The authoritative poem on Tajwīd comprising 107 lines. Memorization is a prerequisite for serious study of the Qirāʾāt. More technically precise than the Tuḥfah, covering the makhārij and ṣifāt in detail.

Ḥirz al-Amānī wa Wajh al-Tahānī (Al-Shāṭibiyyah) — Al-Imām al-Shāṭibī (d. 590 AH) Memorize [Notes] - Topics included in this set of notes are :

  • Faṣl al-Uṣūl — Foundational Principles - The opening section establishing rules common to all seven recitations.
  • The Seven Imāms & Their Rāwīs - Who they are, their transmission lines, and how their differences are encoded in the poem.
  • Topic Chapters & Sūrah-by-Sūrah Variations - The bulk of the poem — variations in specific words across the seven recitations.

Notes in preparation. The great poem of the seven canonical recitations, comprising 1173 lines. Memorizing and studying this text is the classical route to obtaining an ijāzah in the seven recitations.

Reading Recommendations

قائمة المطالعة

Books I have read and found genuinely worth recommending — not exhaustive lists, but books that actually changed how I think or gave me something I could not find elsewhere. Level indicators are rough guides only.

Arabic Language

Sharḥ Ibn ʿAqīl ʿalā Alfiyyat Ibn Mālik Advanced — Ibn ʿAqīl

The standard commentary used alongside the Alfiyyah in traditional study. Dense but indispensable.

Theology & Spirituality

Iḥyāʾ ʿUlūm al-Dīn Advanced — Imām al-Ghazālī

There is no book in Islam after the Qurʾān and Sunnah that combines legal, theological, and spiritual knowledge at this level. Read it slowly, over years, not months.

Ayyuhā al-Walad Introductory — Imām al-Ghazālī

A short letter from al-Ghazālī to a student asking what knowledge is truly for. One of the most important things a student of the sciences can read before going any further.

The Book of Assistance Introductory — Imām al-Ḥaddād

A practical guide to the spiritual life from one of the great scholars of the Bāʿ Alawī tradition.

Islam and the Destiny of Man Introductory — Gai Eaton

The most intellectually serious introduction to Islam written in English.

Traveling Home Introductory — Abdal Hakim Murad

Essays on Islam, modernity, and the interior life. Murad at his most personal.

Sīrah & Biography

Muhammad: His Life Based on the Earliest Sources Introductory — Martin Lings

The finest sīrah available in English. Read this before any other sīrah book.

Al-Shifāʾ bi-Taʿrīf Ḥuqūq al-Muṣṭafā Intermediate — Qāḍī ʿIyāḍ

The definitive classical work on the rights and description of the Prophet ﷺ. Every Muslim should read this at least once.

Ṭabaqāt al-Shāfiʿiyyah al-Kubrā Advanced — Tāj al-Dīn al-Subkī

Biographies of the major scholars of the Shāfiʿī school.

The 11th Hour Introductory — Martin Lings

Lings on the spiritual crisis of the modern world. Short, lucid, and sobering.

Fiqh & Usūl

Islam and Secularism Advanced — Sayyid Naquib al-Attas

Al-Attas's central argument: that secularization represents a specific metaphysical worldview imposed on Islamic thought. Demanding but foundational.

The Islamic Secular Intermediate — Sherman Jackson

Jackson's argument that the Islamic tradition has its own internal resources for navigating the secular.

Ijāzāt

الإجازات

An ijāzah is a written authorization from a qualified scholar certifying that a student has studied a text or transmitted narrations to a sufficient standard, and granting permission to teach or transmit it in turn. The chain of ijāzāt connects every student, through their teachers, back to the Prophet ﷺ.

Ijāzāt will be listed here as they are received, bi-idhnillāh. Each entry will include the name of the authorizing scholar, the text or chain covered, the date, and where possible a scan of the written ijāzah itself.
